What is Hospice in Medical Billing?
-----------------------------------

Hospice is a condition in which providers deliver medical care to patients and their families with chronic diseases, especially those with shorter life spans. Likewise, the caregiver renders medication, psychological, and emotional support. It includes nursing care where the medical staff stays with the patient at their home to render care.

Levels of Hospice Care in Medical Billing
-----------------------------------------

CMS has mentioned four levels of hospice in medical billing based on the daily payment made to providers rendering the services.

### Routine Home Care

It is the standard level of care at which a patient receives care at home to manage a chronic illness. Likewise, it is not continuous care because the patient is not in a severe condition. Routine home care doesn’t only deliver care at the patient’s home. It can be a skilled nursing facility or an assisted living facility.

### Continuous Home Care

It is not an inpatient facility where a patient receives continuous care. Continuous home care is when a nurse stays with the patient to provide medical services. In addition, the home care can be for a limited period or for times when needed.

### General Inpatient Care

A general inpatient care is the level of hospice care when a patient is admitted to the inpatient hospice facility for a specific time. Similarly, this level of hospice care in medical billing is used to analyze the patient’s progress and health status. It further includes chronic pain management, which is a bit harder to treat at the patient’s home.

### Inpatient Respite Care

It is the level of hospice care where a patient stays at the hospital for consecutive days. Respite care is typically used to provide caregivers with rest. A patient receiving inpatient respite care can stay for 5 consecutive days. Likewise, this type of care helps caregivers rest from delivering medical care to patients.

What is Hospice Coinsurance?
----------------------------

Coinsurance is an **[out of pocket in medical billing](https://docsmedicalbilling.com/out-of-pocket-in-medical-billing-a-comprehensive-explanation/)** that is a patient’s responsibility. Hospice coinsurance includes payment for drugs and biologicals. The patient may be required to pay up to 5% of the cost of each prescription drug or biological, but the amount cannot exceed $5 per prescription. Furthermore, no coinsurance applies for general inpatient care.

Which Services Are Included in Hospice?
---------------------------------------

Not all services are included in hospice medical billing. According to the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid, as a provider, you can bill the following items and services to the payer:

*   Services from a hospice-employed physician, nurse practitioner (NP), or other physicians chosen by the patient
*   Nursing care
*   Medical equipment
*   Medical supplies
*   Pain and symptom management drugs
*   Hospice aide and homemaker services
*   Physical therapy
*   Occupational therapy
*   Speech-language pathology services
*   Medical social services
*   Dietary counseling
*   Spiritual counseling
*   Individual and family or just family grief and loss counseling before and after the patient’s death
