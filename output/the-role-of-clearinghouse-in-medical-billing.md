What Services Do Clearinghouses Provide?
----------------------------------------

Clearinghouse eases the process of electronic claim submission to payers. Similarly, healthcare providers can optimize reimbursements and reduce revenue losses. The clearinghouse submits medical claims in a standardized format acceptable to the payers. As a result, it makes the claim submission process efficient and keeps the revenue cycle intact. Every payer has unique requirements when it comes to claim submissions. A clearinghouse makes medical claims more accurate according to payer guidelines for quicker payments. Here are some services clearinghouses offer:

### Claim Scrubbing

This process involves the identification of billing and coding errors and ensuring that medical claims are submitted according to payer guidelines.

### Submitting Claims in Batches

Healthcare providers can use a clearinghouse in medical billing to submit claims in batches. As a result, they can save time and resources.

### Submission to Multiple Payers

A clearinghouse can handle claims submission to multiple payers like Medicare, Medicaid, and all commercial payers.

### Track Medical Claims

Tracking medical claims allows healthcare providers to check the status of claims. Similarly, clearinghouses share regular reports and enable providers to reduce denials.

### Electronic Remittance Advice (ERA)

A clearinghouse submits the ERA to healthcare providers from insurance payers, which contains the details of payments and adjustments.

### Coordination of Benefits (COB)

Managing COB enables providers to get payments from multiple insurance coverage plans. A clearinghouse submits accurate claims to primary and secondary insurance payers.

### HIPAA Compliance and Data Security

Clearinghouses maintain strict compliance with the HIPAA rules to protect patient health data.
