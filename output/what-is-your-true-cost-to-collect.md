# What is Your True Cost-to-Collect?

*Original URL: https://www.medicalbillersandcoders.com/blog/what-is-your-true-cost-to-collect/*

---

Your true Cost-to-Collect is the total expense your organization incurs to collect every dollar of revenue, calculated by dividing total revenue cycle costs by patient service cash collected—and it likely includes hidden expenses you’re not tracking that push you well above the industry-leading benchmark of 2%.

Most medical groups think they know their collection costs, but they’re only seeing part of the picture. While you might be tracking obvious expenses like billing staff salaries, the real number includes dozens of hidden costs that silently erode your EBITDA. Understanding your true cost isn’t just an accounting exercise—it’s the first step toward reclaiming millions in operational efficiency.

Beyond the Obvious: What Really Drives Collection Costs?
--------------------------------------------------------

The formula itself is simple: (Total Revenue Cycle Costs / Total Patient Service Cash Collected) x 100. But the devil lives in defining “total revenue cycle costs.” Most organizations dramatically underestimate this figure.

Your comprehensive Cost-to-Collect must include patient access functions like scheduling and insurance verification, health information management expenses for coding and chart completion, patient accounting costs covering billing and collections, denial management operations, and the often-overlooked IT infrastructure expenses including hardware, software licenses, and servers.

When you add these components honestly, organizations typically discover they’re operating at 3-4% instead of the 2-3% they assumed. High-performing medical groups using advanced automation and strategic partnerships have driven this metric down to 2%, creating a competitive advantage that flows directly to their bottom line.

Revenue Leakage Crisis Hiding in Plain Sight
--------------------------------------------

**Here’s the uncomfortable truth:** while you’re focused on collecting revenue, your inefficient processes are bleeding cash. According to the American Hospital Association, hospitals have absorbed nearly $745 billion in uncompensated care since 2000. By 2022, more than half of US hospitals operated with negative margins.

The billing error epidemic compounds this crisis. The US healthcare industry loses an estimated $125 billion annually from billing mistakes alone. These aren’t just small coding errors—they represent systemic failures in revenue cycle operations that inflate your collection costs while simultaneously reducing revenue capture.

The timing of collections matters more than most leaders realize. An overdue invoice becomes 400-900% more expensive to collect than one within the credit period. Specifically, collecting an invoice 90 days past due costs 50 times more than collecting it during its initial term due to increased labor hours and weighted average cost of capital expenses.

According to USAspending.gov, the federal government obligated approximately $7.6 trillion in healthcare and grant funding in FY 2023, yet an estimated 9-20% remains unspent annually due to administrative inefficiencies—representing nearly $1.5 trillion in lost opportunities. Your revenue cycle likely mirrors these same patterns of leaving money uncollected.
