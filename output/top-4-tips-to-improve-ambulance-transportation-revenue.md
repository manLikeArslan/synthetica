Tips to Improve Ambulance Transportation Revenue:
-------------------------------------------------

### 1\. Record Unusual Circumstances

Every detail, whether it should be mentioned on the claim or not, must be documented. This could include an equipment failure, or transport being declined by the patient or the fact that on-scene time was much longer than expected, etc. Such unusual circumstances are rare, although the plausibility of them happening cannot be declined. In most organizations, mentioning these in additional forms could assist in faster reimbursement.

### 2\. Complete Documentation

Having transitioned from paper to electronic documentation, most EMS systems have proven convenient for recoding data, however, could get challenging while documenting services provided, at the moment. It is important you are completely aware of such systems and mention every piece of information, rather than relying on mere memory. Attending to the patient may need you to fill in details post the treatment, which may end up in some missing details. Hence complete documentation is crucial.

### 3\. Days to Payment

Commercial payers pay forty-five days to post the date of service whereas Medicare pays fourteen days after a claim has been received electronically. It is imperative you have a sound process of follow-up in place, thus helping in having your payments received on time.

### 4\. Avoid Compliance Risks

There is great variation in the commission rates and Ambulance Service providers must be cautious of the rates that seem visibly good. Most EMS providers do not actually check the patient chart and instead rely on an electronic interface for specific fields to be prepopulated which may cause compliance risks.

Ensuring the collection of every dollar for Ambulance Transportation is vital for a profitable service. Being able to reach the patient on time and also ensuring errorless documentation is both critical for timely and complete reimbursement.
