FAQs
----

**1\. Why is accuracy important in radiology billing and coding?**

Accurate billing and coding ensure proper reimbursement and minimize claim denials, which helps improve cash flow and reduce administrative burden.

**2\. What should radiologists do to keep their documentation accurate?**

Radiologists should focus on clear and consistent documentation, ensuring clinical indications, impressions, and findings are accurately reflected in the report.

**3\. What is an Advanced Beneficiary Notice (ABN), and when is it required?**

An ABN is required when Medicare does not cover a service due to medical necessity. It must be signed by the patient to ensure coverage of the professional component fee.

**4\. How can radiology practices manage billing errors and inconsistencies?**

Regularly review exam templates, update protocols, and create procedures to address inconsistencies, such as multiple views, to ensure accurate reimbursement.

**5\. Why should radiology practices educate staff on patient payment encounters?**

Educating staff helps resolve payment issues quickly, improves communication with patients, and reduces delays in processing insurance claims or resolving billing disputes.
