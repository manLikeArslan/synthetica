Critical OB-GYN Coding Areas That Require Expertise
---------------------------------------------------

### Maternity Care

[Billing for maternity services](https://www.medicalbillersandcoders.com/speciality/obgyn-medical-billing-services.html) becomes complex when multiple entities are involved in the same episode of care. Expert billers ensure:

*   Proper use of global maternity codes vs. component billing
*   Correct application of modifiers -24, -25, and -59
*   Accurate trimester-specific coding for complications
*   Appropriate billing for high-risk pregnancy management
*   Coordination between prenatal, delivery, and postpartum care

### Preventive Services

Women’s wellness billing requires precise knowledge of:

*   Annual wellness visit codes and requirements
*   Screening mammography vs. diagnostic mammography
*   Pap smear and HPV testing protocols
*   Contraceptive management coding
*   Preventive service age and frequency limitations

### Surgical Procedures

Complex surgical coding encompasses:

*   Laparoscopic vs. open procedures
*   Correct DRG assignment for inpatient services
*   Technical and professional component separation
*   Bilateral procedure coding
*   Surgical package rules and separately billable services

### Ancillary Services

Each ancillary service has specific CPT codes that accurately describe the procedure or test performed, and modifiers like -TC (Technical Component) and -26 (Professional Component) are essential tools that help provide additional information to payers.

Addressing Common Payer Issues in Texas
---------------------------------------

### Medicare and Medicaid Specifics

Texas OB-GYN practices must navigate both federal Medicare guidelines and Texas-specific Medicaid policies. Medicare payment is made under the Physician Fee Schedule for services furnished in a variety of settings, including physician offices, hospitals, ambulatory surgical centers, skilled nursing facilities, and beneficiaries’ homes.

Key considerations include:

*   Medicare global obstetric package requirements
*   Texas Medicaid perinatal care coordination
*   Dual-eligible patient billing protocols
*   Medicare secondary payer rules
*   Medicaid managed care plan requirements

### Commercial Insurance Challenges

OB/GYN practices typically work with a variety of insurance providers, each with their own set of rules, and navigating through them adds a layer of complexity to billing. Professional billing services maintain:

*   Payer-specific fee schedules and contracts
*   Pre-authorization requirements by payer and procedure
*   Appeal procedures for each insurance company
*   Timely filing deadlines
*   Coordination of benefits protocols
