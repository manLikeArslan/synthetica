Below are the most critical **medical billing KPI formulas** your practice should measure regularly—and what they reveal about your financial performance.

### 1\. Days in A/R

**Formula:** Total A/R ÷ Average Daily Charges  
**Example:** 900,000 ÷ 30,000 = 30 Days

This metric shows how quickly your practice collects payments. A high number may indicate cash flow problems or operational delays.

### **2\. Clean Claim Rate**

**Formula:** (Clean Claims ÷ Total Claims) × 100  
**Example:** (2,280 ÷ 2,400) × 100 = 95%

A high clean claim rate means fewer rejections and faster reimbursements—something MBC prioritizes through automated scrubbing and dedicated account oversight.

### 3\. Denial Rate

**Formula:** (Denied Claims ÷ Total Claims) × 100  
**Example:** (120 ÷ 2,400) × 100 = 5%

Your denial rate is a direct indicator of billing accuracy. MBC minimizes this through proactive denial management and payer-specific insights.

### 4\. Net Collection Rate

**Formula:** (Payments ÷ Adjusted Charges) × 100  
**Example:** (940,000 ÷ 970,000) × 100 = 96.9%

This reveals how much of your expected revenue you’re actually collecting—one of the most telling **medical billing KPI formulas** in your [RCM](https://www.medicalbillersandcoders.com/revenue-management-services.aspx) toolkit.

### 5\. First Pass Resolution Rate

**Formula:** (Claims Paid on First Submission ÷ Total Claims) × 100  
**Example:** (2,200 ÷ 2,400) × 100 = 91.7%

A strong first-pass rate keeps your receivables moving. MBC’s clean claim preparation plays a big role in improving this number.

### 6\. Bad Debt Ratio

**Formula:** (Bad Debt ÷ Total Charges) × 100  
**Example:** (30,000 ÷ 1,000,000) × 100 = 3%

This metric helps practices understand how much revenue is permanently lost, guiding more informed [financial strategies](https://www.cms.gov/training-education/partner-outreach-resources/american-indian-alaska-native/ltss-ta-center/information/ltss-financing/financial-planning-steps).

### 7\. Credit Balance A/R

**Formula:** (Credit Balance A/R ÷ Total A/R) × 100  
**Example:** (45,000 ÷ 900,000) × 100 = 5%

Frequent monitoring of credit balances prevents compliance risks and ensures clean books.

### 8\. Charge Lag Days

**Formula:** Total Days in A/R ÷ Number of Days  
**Example:** 1,800 ÷ 800 = 2.25 Days

Shorter lag times mean faster reimbursement. MBC’s streamlined charge entry process reduces delays.

### 9\. Patient Collection Rate

**Formula:** (Patient Payments ÷ Total Patient Responsibility) × 100  
**Example:** (90,000 ÷ 120,000) × 100 = 75%

This KPI measures your effectiveness at collecting from patients—a growing concern in today’s high-deductible environment.
