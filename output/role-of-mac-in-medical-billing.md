What is Medicare Administrative Contractor (MAC)?
-------------------------------------------------

Medicare Administrative Contractor (MAC) is a private contractor that links the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid (CMS) and healthcare physicians. It manages claim processing for fee-for-service (FFS) beneficiaries to help providers receive accurate and timely reimbursement. Similarly, it ensures payer compliance and conducts medical audits to avoid billing discrepancies.

Types of Medicare Administrative Contractor (MAC)
-------------------------------------------------

There are two types of MACs in medical billing:

*   **Part A/B MACs:** MAC in medical billing manages medical claims for the medical institutions, physicians, and outpatient services.
*   **DME MACs:** Durable Medical Equipment MACs include verifying the medical necessity of medical equipment. It further helps process claims for equipment like wheelchairs, medical devices, prosthetics, etc.

Role of MAC in Medical Billing
------------------------------

### Claims Processing

MAC ensures that Medicare’s payment reaches the right person. It enables providers to submit clean claims by reviewing and verifying the Medicare claims requirements. Similarly, it verifies whether the correct code is used for the services rendered. Hence, it examines the claim submission process throughout medical billing.

### Provider Enrollment

Credentialing is a provider’s enrollment in Medicare so that they receive reimbursement. MAC reviews the credentialing of the provider to ensure that the provider receiving the reimbursement is credentialed with Medicare. Furthermore, they also help new providers with the enrollment process.

### Customer Support Service

MAC in medical billing provides customer support services to providers by guiding them on the medical billing process and updating them on CMS guidelines. Furthermore, it educates providers on coding guidelines to reduce the risk of coding errors while submitting claims.

### Medical Audits

Conducting regular medical audits is vital to streamlining the financial health of medical practices. Similarly, it also conducts a **[TPE audit in healthcare](https://docsmedicalbilling.com/understanding-tpe-audit-in-healthcare/)** to help providers learn the reason behind frequent claim denials.

### Appeal Management

Claim rejection doesn’t mean there will be no reimbursement for the medical services rendered. Providers can seek MAC’s help when appealing for claims submission to the payer. Likewise, the claims denial management experts in MAC resolve the billing and coding errors in the rejected claim.
