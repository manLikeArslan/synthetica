What is Legacy AR in Dermatology Billing?
-----------------------------------------

Legacy AR (Accounts Receivable) refers to aged, unresolved accounts stemming from denied claims, coding errors, or delayed follow-ups. Shockingly, about 60% of denied claims are never resubmitted, leading to substantial revenue losses. Dermatology Billing Tips suggest that practices can address these accounts to reclaim lost revenue and boost cash flow.

Why is Legacy AR Management Crucial for Your Practice?
------------------------------------------------------

*   Significant Revenue Potential: About 30% of healthcare revenue is tied up in aging AR.
*   Time Sensitivity: Claims older than 90 days have a recovery rate of less than 20%.
*   Efficiency Gains: Streamlined AR management speeds up claim resolution by 20-30%, ensuring a healthier revenue cycle.

Managing legacy AR helps prevent your revenue from getting stuck in administrative limbo.

Top Dermatology Billing Tips to Optimize Legacy AR
--------------------------------------------------

### 1\. Analyze Your AR

Break down claims by age, value, and reasons for denial. To improve cash flow quickly, focus first on high-value claims (e.g., over $5,000) that are older than 60 days.

### 2\. Automate Follow-Ups

Automation boosts follow-up efficiency by 25%. Use billing tools with automated reminders and tracking to ensure no claim is overlooked.

### 3\. Ensure Accurate Coding

Coding errors are the cause of claim denials. Certified coders can improve acceptance rates by up to 40%. Stay up-to-date with CPT and ICD-10 changes, especially for procedures like Mohs surgery or cosmetic treatments.

### 4\. Prioritize Denial Management

Prevent nearly 90% of denied claims by tracking and addressing patterns. High-value denials should be appealed promptly to recover revenue.

### 5\. Enhance Patient Communication

Educating patients about their financial responsibilities makes a difference. Offering online payment portals can boost collections by 20%.

### 6\. Conduct Regular Billing Audits

Monthly billing audits can identify inefficiencies. One dermatology practice saved $25,000 annually by reducing errors through consistent audits.
