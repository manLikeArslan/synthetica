Key Components of Medical Billing System
----------------------------------------

### Patient Registration

The first step in the medical billing system is patient registration. This step includes verifying the patient’s data, medical history, and treatment planning. It can be done manually or electronically, but electronic registration is preferable to avoid billing errors.

### Insurance and Eligibility Verification

Eligibility verification means evaluating the patient’s financial responsibility. As a provider, you must verify whether the payer covers the medical expenses of the services you’ll render to your patient. It is crucial because it saves your patients from out-of-pocket costs.

### Medical Coding

The American Medical Association (AMA) has standardized codes for every medical service and medication. Each service has a different code. For example, J codes in medical billing are a subset of HCPCS Level II, used for injections and drugs. Similarly, there are Current Procedural Terminology (CPT) codes, ICD-10, and the HCPCS coding system to reimburse payers for the services rendered.

### Charge Capture

Charge capture in medical billing is the process of charging the exact code for the same medical service. It includes recording billable services rendered to the patient. Likewise, the components of charge capture include procedures, medications, and supplies. As a provider, it is necessary to quote accurate charge capture to receive rightful reimbursement.

### Claim Submission

Submit clean claims to payers by providing accurate documentation. In addition, regulatory and payer compliance is necessary because it enhances the claim acceptance rate. By submitting precise documentation, you can increase your reimbursement.

### Claim Adjudication

The next step in the medical billing system is the claim adjudication process.. In this process, the payer reviews the authenticity of the claim. The payor assesses the claim for the patient’s information to ensure accuracy. Likewise, it evaluates the medical codes in the claim, ensuring they match the Current Procedural Terminology (CPT codes) and the Healthcare Common Procedure Coding System (HCPCS).

### Patient Payment Collection

**[Patient collection](https://docsmedicalbilling.com/8-effective-ways-to-boost-patient-collection-for-your-practice/)** involves sending invoices to patients for the services they receive at the hospital. In addition, it evaluates whether the provider has received payment for the medical services rendered. Likewise, multiple payment options such as credit/debit cards, cash, and checks increase collection significantly.

### Follow-Ups

Follow-ups are for denied claims and pending payment. Expert billers stay in touch with payers and patients regarding claim status, which reduces payment delays and issues. Furthermore, timely follow-ups are vital to streamlining overall revenue.
