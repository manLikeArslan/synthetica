What Causes Revenue Leakage?
----------------------------

Revenue leakage is the preventable loss of income due to errors or delays in the medical billing and collection process. Common causes include:

*   Inaccurate or incomplete coding
*   Missed billable services
*   Claims submitted with errors or missing documentation
*   Ineffective denial management
*   Lack of follow-up on aging accounts

In large practices with multiple departments and complex billing needs, these issues often go unnoticed until they significantly impact financial stability.

The Financial Impact on Large Practices:
----------------------------------------

Revenue leakage can have a substantial impact on large, high-volume practices. Missing just $20 in charges per patient visit across 5,000 visits monthly adds up to **$100,000 per month—or $1.2 million annually**.

Additionally, industry data shows that up to **20% of claims are denied** on the first submission. Many of these claims go uncorrected and uncollected, further contributing to lost revenue.
