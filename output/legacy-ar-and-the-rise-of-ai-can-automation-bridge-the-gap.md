**Understanding Legacy AR**
---------------------------

Legacy accounts receivable (AR) are overdue payments for medical services provided in the past. Physicians should understand its relevance to financial health. Assume a patient had therapy six months ago but has not paid. This outstanding sum remains in legacy AR, affecting cash flow and preventing budget planning. It indicates previously given services that have yet to be reimbursed.

Left uncontrolled, it accumulates, putting a burden on resources and revenue. Physicians establish regular revenue streams and operational stability by resolving historical AR promptly with appropriate billing and collections techniques. It’s similar to managing a garden—nurturing existing plants (current revenue) while removing weeds (old AR) to maintain growth.
