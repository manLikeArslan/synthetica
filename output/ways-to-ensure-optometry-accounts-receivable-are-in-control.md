FAQs
----

**1\. Why is managing accounts receivable (AR) important for optometry practices?**

Effective AR management is crucial for maintaining cash flow and preventing financial burdens, ensuring that practices can operate smoothly and profitably.

**2\. What should be the target amount for accounts receivable?**

A good target for AR is generally less than one month’s gross income, depending on the amount billed to insurance. This helps maintain a healthy cash flow.

**3\. How can practices ensure timely payments from patients?**

Establish clear payment policies, such as requiring full payment at the time of service, and communicate these expectations to patients upfront.

**4\. What role does staff play in managing AR?**

Designating a staff member to regularly review AR reports and follow up with overdue accounts is essential for keeping AR under control and improving collection rates.

**5\. How often should practices send billing statements?**

Practices should send statements consistently, ideally on the same date each month, to keep patients informed of outstanding balances and encourage prompt payments.
