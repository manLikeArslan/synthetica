### **Q1: What is the First Pass Resolution Rate (FPRR), and why should I care as a physician?**

The FPRR measures the percentage of claims approved and paid upon the first submission. Industry benchmarks suggest that an FPRR of **90% or higher** is ideal.

*   **Why It’s Important for Physicians:**

*   *   Practices with an FPRR below **80%** typically experience delays in revenue collection and higher administrative costs.
    *   Reduces rework costs, which can average **$25–$50 per claim** for resubmissions.

*   **How a Medical Biller Helps:**

*   *   Regularly updates CPT and ICD-10 codes to maintain accuracy.
    *   Provides training to minimize submission errors and achieve FPRRs above **90%**.

### **Q2: How does monitoring the Claim Denial Rate benefit my practice?**

The Claim Denial Rate is the percentage of claims rejected by insurers. A **denial rate under 5%** is considered efficient; rates over **10%** require immediate intervention.

*   **Why It Matters:**

*   *   Each denied claim costs practices an average of **$118** to appeal or correct.
    *   High denial rates can delay revenue collection by up to **30–60 days**.

*   **What to Look for in a Biller:**

*   *   Expertise in reducing denial rates to **below 5%** through proactive claim scrubbing.
    *   Use of [denial management](https://www.medicalbillersandcoders.com/revenue-management-services.aspx?DivId=denial-management-appeals) software to analyze trends and implement corrective measures.

### **Q3: What are Days in Accounts Receivable (AR), and how do they impact my practice’s finances?**

Days in AR is the average number of days it takes to collect payments. Industry standards suggest keeping AR days below **40 days**, with the best-performing practices maintaining it at **30 days or less**.

*   **How It Affects Physicians:**

*   *   Practices with AR days exceeding **60 days** often experience cash flow constraints.
    *   Reducing AR days by **10 days** can free up significant cash flow, equivalent to **10–15% of monthly revenue**.

*   **How a Biller Can Help:**

*   *   By prioritizing accurate [documentation](https://www.medicalbillersandcoders.com/articles/outsource-medical-billing/wound-care-documentation-examples.html) and timely follow-ups, an experienced biller can reduce AR days by **20–30%**.
    *   Implements workflows that ensure payments are received within the industry standard of **30–40 days**.

### **Q4: What is a Clean Claim Rate, and why should I ask about it?**

The Clean Claim Rate measures the percentage of claims submitted without errors. Industry standards suggest maintaining a rate of **95% or higher** for optimal efficiency.

*   **Why It’s Vital for Physicians:**

*   *   Practices with a clean claim rate below **90%** face significant delays, often adding **10–14 days** to the revenue cycle.
    *   Insurers process clean claims **30% faster** compared to error-ridden claims.

*   **What a Skilled Biller Offers:**

*   *   Utilizes advanced Revenue Cycle Management (RCM) software to catch and correct errors.
    *   Implements processes to achieve and sustain clean claim rates above **95%**.

### **Q5: What is the Net Collection Rate, and how does it reflect the biller’s effectiveness?**

The Net Collection Rate reflects the percentage of total collectible revenue received by a practice. A rate of **96% or higher** is ideal, while rates below **90%** indicate issues with billing processes or payer [reimbursement](https://www.medicalbillersandcoders.com/blog/increasing-wound-care-reimbursements/).

*   **Why It’s Crucial:**

*   *   A low net collection rate can cost practices **$10,000–$50,000 annually** in uncollected revenue.
    *   The effective collection ensures you receive nearly all allowable revenue.

*   **What to Ask a Biller:**

*   *   How they reconcile payments against payer contracts to address underpayments.
    *   Strategies they employ to promptly manage denials or disputes, ensuring a net collection rate above **96%**.

*   **Examples of Wound Care Codes:**
