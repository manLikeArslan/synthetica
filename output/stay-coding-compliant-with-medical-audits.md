Basics of Coding Compliant with Medical Audits
----------------------------------------------

A coding audit is a review of a medical office’s coding practices conducted by reviewing patient medical records. Medical record audits target and evaluates procedural and diagnosis code selection as determined by physician documentation for completeness and accuracy.

Medical audits cover: assessing the proper use of CPT codes; determining the correct places of service; finding out missing and/or incorrect use of modifiers; detecting incorrect diagnoses; documentation accuracy; identifying any bundling or cluster issues.  

Accurate coding by Physicians is critical to getting paid for the services provided and for avoiding external audits by Medicare and other private Payers. The easiest and probably best way is to audit a select sample of charts. Improving the quality of your coding through regular coding audits keeps your office running efficiently. These benefits ensure that you use your staff’s time efficiently, and cash flow is maximized. Regular coding audits reduce overpayments and underpayments, lower the rate of rejections and denials, keep your practice compliant, encourage your staff to focus on quality coding, and ultimately maximize your profit.

Regular coding audits educate and improve your practice and ensure that your practice will not be flagged for fraud, waste, or abuse.

FAQs
----

****1\. What is a medical coding compliance audit?****

A medical coding compliance audit is a review of a practice’s coding processes, which involves evaluating patient medical records, procedural and diagnosis code selection, and ensuring that coding is accurate, complete, and compliant with industry standards. It helps identify errors such as incorrect CPT codes, missing modifiers, incorrect diagnoses, and bundling issues.

****2\. Why is a coding audit important for my practice?****

Coding audits are crucial because they help ensure that your practice stays compliant with changing medical billing guidelines, minimizes the risk of claim denials, and prevents costly payer investigations. They identify areas of improvement in coding practices and reduce the chance of underpayments or overpayments, ensuring that you receive accurate reimbursement.

****3\. How often should coding audits be conducted?****

It is recommended to conduct coding audits regularly, either monthly or quarterly. The frequency depends on your practice’s size, the volume of claims, and the complexity of services provided. Regular audits help keep your practice compliant, improve coding accuracy, and avoid discrepancies that could result in claim denials or payer investigations.

****4\. What are the benefits of regular coding audits?****

Regular coding audits help ensure compliance with current regulations, reduce claim rejections and denials, educate your staff on coding best practices, and maximize reimbursement. By identifying coding errors early, audits improve the accuracy of claims, enhance cash flow, and reduce the risk of costly investigations.

****5\. How does a coding audit reduce compliance risk?****

By identifying and correcting errors in coding practices before they become significant issues, regular coding audits reduce compliance risks. They help prevent practices from being flagged for potential fraud, waste, or abuse. This proactive approach ensures that your practice avoids costly investigations and maintains accurate, compliant billing practices.
