**Payment Procedure of Ambulatory Surgical Centers**
----------------------------------------------------

For ASC to be reimbursed, the service provided must be a medical necessity. The medical procedure performed must meet certain requirements agreed upon by both the patient and the provider.

**Does ASC require any specialty billing?**
-------------------------------------------

Ambulatory surgery service charging typically does not focus on a specific medical specialty or on particular procedures, services, or diagnoses. So, billing is unlike any medical specialty billing. But, [Ambulatory Surgical Center medical billing](https://bit.ly/3NBYzsX) uses the same techniques, codes, and guidelines as other medical entities. For example, ASC bills use CMS-1500 claim forms, while hospitals use UB-04 claim forms for reimbursement.

**What type of services get covered?**
--------------------------------------

Depending on the medical facility’s status, the ASC guidelines for covered services are outlined. When an ASC is fully credentialed, it can perform numerous procedures, making it the fastest-growing healthcare service.

**Services not covered in ASC are:**
------------------------------------

*   Non-implantable durable medical equipment
*   Artificial arm, leg, and eyes
*   Physician services
*   Independent laboratory services
*   Ambulance services
*   Leg, back, neck, and arm braces
*   Prosthetic devices

**Are procedural codes different?**
-----------------------------------

As far as medical billing for ASC is concerned, procedural codes must be precise to ensure efficient, sufficient reimbursement. These codes vary depending on the procedures being performed and the type of medical facility where services are provided. The two vital codes used here are the ICD-10-PCS, which covers inpatient procedures, and the Current Procedural Terminology (CPT), which gives information regarding services rendered. These are applied along with [ICD-10](https://www.medicalbillersandcoders.com/ICD-10.aspx) diagnosis codes.

 **Multi-specialty billing**

Multiple specialties point towards a variety of underlying causes, diagnoses, and procedures. Keep in mind that charging for ASC multi-specialty procedures is complicated, but it is one of the fastest-growing healthcare services. FAQs
----

### 1\. **Why is ASC medical billing and coding different from other healthcare specialties?**

*   ASC billing does not focus on specific medical specialties but covers a variety of procedures across multiple specialties. Although ASC billing uses similar coding techniques and guidelines as other medical entities, it involves specific requirements and forms, such as the [CMS](http://cms.gov/)\-1500, rather than the UB-04 used by hospitals.

### 2\. **What are some common services that are not covered under ASC billing?**

*   Services not typically covered under ASC billing include non-implantable durable medical equipment, prosthetic devices, artificial limbs, physician services, independent lab services, ambulance services, and specific braces for limbs, back, and neck.

### 3\. **How does outsourcing ASC billing help improve profitability?**

*   Outsourcing ASC billing and coding helps reduce financial complexities, improve claim accuracy, and increase profitability by ensuring compliance with billing standards. Experienced billing teams handle claims, denial management, and A/R follow-up, allowing ASCs to focus on providing quality patient care.

### 4\. **What coding standards are essential for ASC billing?**

*   ASC billing requires precise coding with CPT codes for outpatient procedures, ICD-10-PCS for inpatient procedures, and ICD-10 diagnosis codes. Accurate coding is essential to avoid claim denials and ensure correct reimbursement for the services provided.

### 5\. **What are the requirements for an ASC to bill and receive reimbursements from CMS?**

*   To bill CMS, ASCs must be fully credentialed, qualify as a medically necessary facility, and meet Medicare and Medicaid standards. The procedures performed must meet specific requirements agreed upon by both the patient and provider to ensure compliance and eligibility for reimbursement.
