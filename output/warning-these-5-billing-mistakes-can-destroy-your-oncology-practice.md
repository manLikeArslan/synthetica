FAQs

### 1\. What are the common errors in oncology billing that affect reimbursement?

Common errors include delayed claim submission, improper handling of Explanation of Benefits (EOB), failure to follow up on aging reports, and neglecting clearinghouse reports. These mistakes can lead to denials and delayed payments.

### 2\. Why is timely claim submission important in oncology billing?

Submitting claims within the payer’s filing limit is crucial to avoid denials. Claims filed late can be rejected, which negatively impacts the practice’s revenue cycle and accounts receivable (A/R) days.

### 3\. How can misunderstandings of EOB impact oncology billing?

Incorrectly reading the Explanation of Benefits (EOB) can lead to underpayment or denial of claims. A thorough understanding is needed to properly resubmit claims for accurate reimbursement.

### 4\. Why should oncology practices regularly review aging reports?

Aging reports highlight overdue claims that need follow-up. Regular review ensures no outstanding claims are missed, improving the likelihood of timely payment and reducing revenue loss.

Outsourcing oncology billing to specialized agencies ensures accurate, timely submissions, regular claim follow-ups, and adherence to billing guidelines, which helps reduce errors and increases overall revenue collection.
