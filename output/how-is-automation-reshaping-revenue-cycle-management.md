**What’s revenue cycle management in clinical practice?**
---------------------------------------------------------

[Revenue cycle management (RCM)](https://www.medicalbillersandcoders.com/revenue-management-services.aspx) in clinical practice is the system of coping with the financial factors of patient care, from appointment scheduling to price collection. It includes numerous stages, including verifying coverage insurance, properly coding operations and diagnoses, submitting claims to insurance organizations, and following up on claim denials or unpaid patient balances.

**Traditional Billing vs. Automated Reshaping Revenue Cycle Management**
------------------------------------------------------------------------

In traditional medical billing, a patient visits a physician’s office for a consultation. After the visit, the physician manually files the patient’s information, prognosis, and methods performed. An administrative team manually inputs this fact into the billing system, generates a claim, and submits it to the insurance organization. Any mistakes or missing information require additional manual follow-up, leading to delays in repayment and potential revenue loss for the health practitioner.

With automation, a patient’s records, analysis, and procedures are captured electronically throughout the visit using an electronic health record (EHR) device. This information seamlessly integrates with the billing software program, automatically producing a claim based on predefined rules and coding recommendations.

The claim is electronically submitted to the insurance company, with automated checks for errors or missing information. Bills are processed quicker, and real-time analytics provide insights into revenue trends and optimization opportunities, ultimately improving the doctor’s financial health and practice efficiency.
