Best Practice 4: Document Medical Necessity Comprehensively
-----------------------------------------------------------

Payers in Maryland increasingly scrutinize medical necessity for high-cost oncology services. Claims must be supported by documentation that clearly establishes why specific treatments, drugs, or procedures were appropriate for the patient’s condition. Insufficient documentation is a leading cause of oncology claim denials.

### Documentation Best Practices:

Every oncology claim should be supported by diagnosis codes that justify the services billed, treatment plans that explain therapeutic rationale, and clinical notes that demonstrate medical decision-making. For novel or high-cost therapies, additional supporting documentation—such as clinical trial data, peer-reviewed literature, or payer-specific coverage policies—may be necessary.
