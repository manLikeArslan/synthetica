# What Can Your ASC Do Today to Avoid Costly Billing Errors in Q3?

---

To prevent costly billing errors in Q3, Ambulatory Surgery Centers (ASCs) should consider taking these important steps:

1.  **Always verify your insurance eligibility** before procedures to avoid billing errors and denials.
2.  **Review charge capture very carefully** so nothing is missed or entered incorrectly in claim submission.
3.  **Keep your billing and coding team updated** on the latest CPT, ICD-10 codes, and payer policies.
4.  **Use claim scrubbing tools** to catch errors early, before claims go out the door.
5.  **Make sure all documentation is clear and complete** to support each claim.
6.  **Monitor denial trends every week** and fix any recurring issues right away.
7.  **Submit claims on time** to avoid rejections due to missed deadlines.

Following these steps can help your ASC protect revenue and boost the success of your claims.

**Prepare your ASC now to comply with the latest CMS payment updates, avoid documentation pitfalls, and eliminate the billing mistakes that can trigger audits and revenue losses.**

**Recent CMS Updates Impacting ASCs:**
--------------------------------------

The Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) has made several important changes that affect how ASCs are reimbursed in 2025. Here are some of the key updates physicians need to know:

*   ### **ASC Payment Rate Updates**
    

CMS has finalized increases in ASC payment rates linked to the hospital market basket. This means higher reimbursement—but only if your coding and documentation fully meet compliance standards.

*   ### **Expanded Covered Procedures**
    

CMS has added more procedures to the ASC Covered Procedures List (CPL). While this creates growth opportunities, it also means more scrutiny over whether cases meet criteria for ASC billing instead of outpatient hospital billing.

*   ### **Prior Authorization Requirements**
    

CMS and many commercial payers have increased prior authorization requirements for certain high-volume or high-cost procedures. Failing to secure authorizations will result in automatic denials.

*   ### **Heightened Program Integrity Audits**
    

CMS has ramped up audits targeting ASCs with unusually high reimbursement or coding variances. Even honest mistakes can trigger recoupments or penalties.

**What Physicians Fear Most About ASC Billing—and How to Overcome It**
----------------------------------------------------------------------

*   ### **Denials From Incomplete Documentation**
    

Surgeons worry that even a missing signature or insufficient operative note detail will cause claim denials or underpayments.

*   ### **Revenue Loss From Coding Errors**
    

Physicians fear that incorrect use of modifiers—especially -73, -74, and -59—will not only delay payments but also flag them for audits.

*   ### **Compliance Penalties**
    

With audits increasing, clinicians are anxious about being penalized for patterns they don’t even realize exist, like overuse of certain codes or billing without proper authorization.

*   ### **Rising Administrative Burdens**
    

Doctors are concerned that administrative work is pulling them away from patient care, yet they still fail to prevent mistakes that affect their income.

FAQs
----

******1\. What steps can ASCs take to avoid billing errors in Q3 2025?******

To avoid billing errors, ASCs should verify insurance eligibility, update CPT/ICD-10 codes, review charge capture, scrub claims, monitor denials weekly, and ensure timely submissions.

****2\. What are the latest CMS billing updates for ASCs in 2025?****

CMS has increased ASC payment rates, expanded the Covered Procedures List (CPL), tightened prior authorization rules, and ramped up program integrity audits.

****3\. How do prior authorization requirements affect ASC billing in 2025?****

Missing prior authorizations for high-cost or high-volume procedures can result in immediate denials. CMS and commercial payers have increased scrutiny in this area.

****4\. What are common billing fears among ASC physicians?****

Physicians often fear denials from incomplete documentation, revenue loss from coding mistakes, compliance penalties from audits, and administrative burdens that affect patient care.

****5\. How can ASCs stay compliant with new CMS rules?****

ASCs should regularly train billing staff, perform coding audits, track policy updates, and improve documentation to align with CMS compliance standards.

****6\. Why is charge capture important in ASC billing?****

Accurate charge capture ensures that every service rendered is billed correctly. Missing or incorrect charges can lead to lost revenue or claim rejections.

****7\. How can claim scrubbing tools help ASCs in Q3?****

Claim scrubbers automatically detect coding errors, missing data, or non-compliant entries before submission, reducing denials and speeding up reimbursement.

****8\. What types of procedures were added to the ASC Covered Procedures List (CPL)?****

CMS has added more complex and outpatient-suitable surgeries to the CPL, allowing ASCs to bill for them—provided documentation justifies their inclusion.

****9\. How can ASCs manage documentation to reduce denials?****

Surgeons should use standardized templates, complete operative notes thoroughly, and include all required signatures to meet medical necessity and compliance standards.

****10\. What is the best way for ASCs to handle Medicare audits?****

Maintain organized records, conduct internal audits, respond promptly to CMS requests, and seek expert help to avoid penalties during audits.

****11\. Why are modifiers like -73, -74, and -59 critical in ASC billing?****

Incorrect usage of these modifiers can lead to denials or audits. They must accurately reflect procedure status and surgical circumstances as per CMS guidelines.

****12\. How often should ASCs monitor denial trends?****

Weekly monitoring helps identify recurring issues early, allowing billing teams to correct patterns and prevent revenue loss before they escalate.

MBC supports ASCs by handling CMS update tracking, coding accuracy, prior authorization, documentation training, and audit defense to reduce denials and boost compliance.
