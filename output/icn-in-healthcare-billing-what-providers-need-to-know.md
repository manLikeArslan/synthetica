What is ICN in Healthcare Billing?
----------------------------------

Internal Control Number is a claim identification number for physicians’ medical services, which is different for every claim. It is a 13-digit number, but the format may vary depending on the payor’s guidelines. ICN is listed on EOB in medical billing to reference the claim. Similarly, ERA in medical billing also includes ICN to reconcile payments and adjustments.

Format of ICN in Healthcare Billing
-----------------------------------

An ICN has 13 digits. It consists of numerical values. Every digit has its meaning. For example, the ICN number for a claim is 2212333456789. Let’s break down the meaning of every digit:

*   First two digits (22) → Year the claim was received (e.g., 2022)
*   Next two digits (12) → Claim submission type (e.g., 12 = electronically submitted claim)
*   Next nine digits (333456789) → Unique sequence assigned by the payor to identify the claim

Importance of ICN in Healthcare Billing
---------------------------------------

ICN helps track every claim a healthcare provider submits to the insurance companies. Its presence on **[EOB in medical billing](https://docsmedicalbilling.com/eob-in-medical-billing-what-patients-need-to-know/)** also helps to reference the original claim. Furthermore, an accurate ICN helps in tracking a claim after its rejection. It enhances the audit and compliance process for billing, leading to limited billing errors.

Common Issues with ICN in Healthcare Billing
--------------------------------------------

### Mismatched ICN

Sometimes, the healthcare staff assigns numerous claims to a single identification number. It causes a mismatch when the payor tries to trace the original claim on ERA.

### Lost ICN in Healthcare Billing

The lost ICN is yet another issue. Missing ICN can cause billing errors. In this case, the provider may need to correct and resubmit the claim.

### ICN Format Inaccuracy

There’s a specific format for ICN. Any inaccuracy in the format leads to claim denials. Therefore, it is vital to follow the format of the ICN as per the payor’s instructions.

Conclusion
----------

ICN in healthcare billing is a unique identifier assigned by the payer after the claim has been submitted and processed. Its format and length can vary slightly depending on the specific payer or claims processing contractor. Different payers may use different conventions for the ICN, so providers should refer to the individual payer’s guidelines for exact ICN format details.
