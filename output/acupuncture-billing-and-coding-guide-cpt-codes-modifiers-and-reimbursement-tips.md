# Acupuncture Billing and Coding Guide CPT Codes, Modifiers, and Reimbursement Tips

Acupuncture is a widely recognized alternative therapy that involves inserting thin needles into specific points on the body to alleviate pain and promote healing. While it has gained popularity in the medical field, billing and coding for acupuncture services can be complex. To ensure accurate reimbursement and compliance with **CMS guidelines**, medical providers must use correct **CPT codes, modifiers, and billing practices**.

Understanding Acupuncture CPT Codes
-----------------------------------

Current Procedural Terminology (CPT) codes are essential for reporting acupuncture services to **Medicare, Medicaid, and private insurance payers**. The commonly used CPT codes for acupuncture include:

*   **97810** – Acupuncture, one or more needles, without electrical stimulation; initial 15 minutes of personal one-on-one contact with the patient.
*   **97811** – Each additional 15 minutes of personal one-on-one contact with the patient, without electrical stimulation (Use in conjunction with 97810).
*   **97813** – Acupuncture, one or more needles, with electrical stimulation; initial 15 minutes of personal one-on-one contact with the patient.
*   **97814** – Each additional 15 minutes of personal one-on-one contact with the patient, with electrical stimulation (Use in conjunction with 97813).

It is crucial to **document the time spent with the patient** to justify billing for additional units.

Essential Modifiers for Acupuncture Billing
-------------------------------------------

Modifiers help specify **procedural details** and avoid claim denials. The common acupuncture billing modifiers include:

*   **GP** – Services delivered under an outpatient physical therapy plan.
*   **KX** – Indicates services that exceed the therapy threshold.
*   **59** – Distinct procedural service when separate services are performed on the same day.
*   **25** – Significant, separately identifiable evaluation and management (E/M) service by the same provider on the same day.

Using the correct modifiers ensures [**clean claim submission**](https://www.medicalbillersandcoders.com/revenue-management-services.aspx?DivId=denial-management-appeals) and timely reimbursement.

Reimbursement Tips for Acupuncture Billing
------------------------------------------

### 1\. **Verify Patient Coverage**

Before providing acupuncture services, verify the patient’s insurance **coverage and limitations**. Not all **Medicare or private insurers** cover acupuncture, except in specific cases like chronic low back pain.

### 2\. **Accurate Documentation**

Ensure complete documentation, including:

*   **Diagnosis codes (ICD-10-CM) supporting medical necessity**
*   **Time spent per session**
*   **Treatment notes and patient response**

### 3\. **Avoid Upcoding and Underbilling**

Always bill for the exact services provided to prevent **claim denials or audits**. If additional services are provided, ensure **proper use of add-on codes**.

### 4\. **Stay Updated with CMS and Insurance Guidelines**

CMS and private insurers frequently update policies regarding **acupuncture billing and coding**. Providers must keep track of these changes to maintain compliance.

### 5\. **Appeal Denied Claims**

If a claim is denied, review the **explanation of benefits (EOB)** and submit an appeal with supporting documentation.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)
---------------------------------

**1\. **Does Medicare cover acupuncture services?****

Yes, **Medicare Part B** covers acupuncture for **chronic low back pain** (up to 12 visits in 90 days). Coverage may extend if significant improvement is noted.

**2\. **What are the most commonly denied acupuncture claims?****

Claims are often denied due to **incorrect coding, lack of medical necessity, or missing modifiers**.

**3\. **Can acupuncture be billed alongside evaluation and management (E/M) services?****

Yes, but **modifier 25** must be appended to the E/M code to indicate a separate, significant service.

**4\. **Which private insurance companies cover acupuncture?****

Coverage varies by provider. Some major insurers like **Aetna, Cigna, and UnitedHealthcare** cover acupuncture under specific conditions.

**5\. **What documentation is required for acupuncture claims?****

Providers must document **diagnosis codes, time spent, treatment plan, and patient response** to the therapy.
