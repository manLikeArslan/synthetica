What is A Clearinghouse in Medical Billing?
-------------------------------------------

A clearinghouse develops an error-free connection between the payer and the healthcare provider. It checks claims and submits them for approval to the insurance payers. It stores information on every claim, enabling them to make electronic funds transfer (EFT) payments. Due to this, many claims are paid on time, and the provider doesn’t have to wait for multiple attempts to submit claims. In addition, the clearinghouse ensures accurate information in the medical claim.

Key Features of Clearinghouse in Medical Billing
------------------------------------------------

### Eligibility Verification

The patient eligibility verification is the first step before submitting claims. Along with the patient’s information, this step verifies insurance status, coverage limits, and out-of-pocket costs. Accurate information increases the chances of claims acceptance.

### Claim Receipt

Healthcare providers send claims to the clearinghouse in medical billing for review. They can send claims manually or through software.

### Claim Scrubbing

The clearinghouse performs **[claim scrubbing in medical billing](https://docsmedicalbilling.com/understanding-claims-scrubbing-in-medical-billing/)** and checks for errors. Claim scrubbing is a process in which the claim scrubber checks the claims for errors and inaccuracy. If there’s any error, the claim is rejected. However, if the claim is accurate, the provider receives reimbursement.

### Claim Standardization

After the claim has been corrected, the clearinghouse converts it into a standardized format to align with payer guidelines. It is also essential to comply with HIPAA policy. This ensures successful claim acceptance.

### Claims Transmission

In this step, the experts transfer claims electronically to the payer for a faster reply and await their response. E-claims are easier to send than traditional paper claims.

### Receipt Confirmation

After submitting the claim to the payer, the clearinghouse staff informs the healthcare provider about the process, ensuring transparency in the claims submission process. Transparency is the key feature behind a successful claim process.

### Payer Response

After review, the payer denies, refuses, or accepts the claim. When the payer agrees with the claim in one go, the payment is successfully transferred to the provider’s account for the services rendered.

### Reports and Analytics

The clearinghouse may share insights into claim submission errors. It helps analyze submission patterns, success rates, and denials.
