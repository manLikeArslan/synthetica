Key CMS 2025 Updates for Emergency Medicine Billing
---------------------------------------------------

1.  ### Revised Evaluation & Management (E/M) Coding Guidelines
    

*   E/M level selection now focuses on Medical Decision Making (MDM) and time spent on patient care.
*   Time-based billing requires clear documentation of critical care duration.
*   CMS emphasizes clarity in history and physical exam documentation.

MBC Solution: We ensure accurate E/M coding and thorough documentation to prevent downcoding and underpayments.

2.  ### Increased Scrutiny on Modifier 25 & 59
    

*   Modifier 25 (E/M service with a procedure) must have distinct documentation.
*   Modifier 59 (separate service) is a top audit target.

MBC ensures proper modifier usage to avoid audits and claim denials.

3.  ### Critical Care Billing Updates (99291 & 99292)
    

*   CMS clarified that 99291 (first 30-74 minutes) requires detailed documentation.
*   Additional 99292 units require precise time tracking.

MBC Strategy: Our team reviews time-based documentation to prevent reimbursement cuts.

4.  ### Telehealth Expansion for Emergency Medicine
    

*   CMS continues covering select telehealth services for ED providers.
*   ED telehealth visits (CPT 99281-99285) require real-time audio-video communication.

MBC ensures proper billing for tele-emergency services.

Key Components of Emergency Medicine Billing
--------------------------------------------

1.  ### Proper Use of E/M Codes for Emergency Visits
    

ED visits are categorized based on complexity and MDM levels:

*   ServiceCPT CodeMDM Complexity
*   Level 1 ED Visit 99281 Minimal
*   Level 2 ED Visit 99282 Low
*   Level 3 ED Visit 99283 Moderate
*   Level 4 ED Visit 99284 High
*   Level 5 ED Visit 99285 Critical

MBC ensures proper code selection to maximize reimbursements.

2.  ### Facility Fee vs. Professional Fee Billing
    

Emergency medicine billing involves:

*   Facility fees (charged by the hospital for ED resources)
*   Professional fees (charged by physicians for services rendered)

MBC ensures accurate billing for both components.

3.  ### Bundled Services & Modifier Usage
    

*   Laceration repairs, fracture care, and procedural sedation are bundled unless modifiers justify separate billing.
*   Common ED modifiers include:

*   *   Modifier 25 – Separates an E/M visit from a procedure
    *   Modifier 57 – Decision for surgery in ED
    *   Modifier 59 – Distinct procedural service

MBC prevents modifier misuse to reduce audit risks.
