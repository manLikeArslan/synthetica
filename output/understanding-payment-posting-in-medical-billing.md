# Understanding Payment Posting in Medical Billing

*Original URL: https://docsmedicalbilling.com/understanding-payment-posting-in-medical-billing/*

---

When transaction tracking is done to ease the payment process, it is called payment posting. It further evaluates whether the payment has been smoothly transferred to the provider’s account. Additionally, in this process, a track record of transferred, delayed, and rejected payments is also balanced to optimize the revenue cycle. Moreover, the healthcare staff can **[outsource medical billing and coding](https://docsmedicalbilling.com/medical-billing-and-coding-services/)** to generate optimal revenue. So, let’s get into the details now!

Types of Payment Posting Method
-------------------------------

There are two types of methods: manual posting and auto posting. It’s like a traditional recording of payment where the staff fills out the information. Moreover, it is a time-consuming process and prone to errors and typos. On the other hand, auto posting is done with the help of software, and staff automates the payment posting process. There’s no need to fill in the information individually.

Steps of Payment Posting Process
--------------------------------

### Receiving Payments

There are multiple ways of receiving payments, such as credits/debits, checks, electronic transfers, and cash. In addition, it is crucial to record all the ways to pay, and for this, the system must be logged with a valid account.

### Match Payments with Claims

After receiving the payments, it is necessary to cross-check them to ensure validity and accuracy. It includes matching the invoices and account numbers with relevant accounts. In addition, healthcare providers submit claims to payers to process payments.

### Handling Adjustments and Denials

It is important to handle adjustments and denials after receiving and matching the payments. In this step, rectification of claims denials and appeal submission is done through denials management. Delays are a hurdle for the payment process. Therefore, managing them timely is necessary to obtain optimal revenue.

### Reconciling Accounts

The next step is payment reconciliation, in which payment verification is done to check that the provider has received an accurate payment. The verification is done by contacting insurance companies, government programs, and patients.

### Balancing Reports

A complete payment posting report is analyzed to identify the discrepancies. After resolving all the issues, a balanced payment report is maintained to keep track of all the payments.

### Continuous Monitoring

To increase and optimize overall revenue, healthcare providers should monitor payment reconciliation.

*   Errors can occur while entering data.
*   Misunderstanding of EOB/ERA details.
*   Delayed information from insurance companies.
*   Frequent claims denials.

Benefits of Accurate Payment Posting
------------------------------------

### Improved Cashflow

Billing accuracy enables providers to receive on-time payments for medical services. A significant improvement in cash flow is noticed, and delays are reduced. In addition, it can prevent revenue loss.

### Reduce Denials

Claim denials are limited when the payment transaction seamlessly uses authentic and secure software. So, Providers don’t have to face errors and rejections.

### Patient Satisfaction

When patients receive a thorough report of their payments in which every detail is mentioned, it enhances patient satisfaction.

### Optimal Revenue Management

Every dollar that comes and goes in a healthcare organization revolves around revenue cycle management. Therefore, an optimal revenue cycle is maintained by accurately utilizing the payment posting process.

Common Metrics to Monitor
-------------------------

There’s a need to monitor some metrics thoroughly:

*   First-pass payment resolution rate
*   Percentage of denied claims
*   Days in accounts receivable (AR)
*   Error rates

Conclusion
----------

To sum up, payment posting evaluates and examines the cash flow of providers and payers. This process ensures timely cash delivery into the provider’s account for the services rendered.
