DME (durable medical equipment) contains devices and equipment prescribed by healthcare professionals to assist patients in their recovery.

Durable medical equipment refers to things or services that intend to endure five years or longer. The category also covers oxygen tents, nebulizers, CPAP, catheters, hospital beds, wheelchairs, and other healthcare-related services. Understanding Denials in DME Medical Billing:
---------------------------------------------

Your clinic may experience an increase in claim denials if DME medical billing criteria fluctuate and payors’ medical coverage varies. To assist in collecting as many claims as possible, here are our three best recommendations for appealing for a **higher collection in DME**.

*   ### **Redetermination**
    

When appealing a **[DME claim denial](https://www.medicalbillersandcoders.com/medical-billing-companies/dme-billing-services.aspx)**, the worst thing you can do is send in documentation with errors. This tells an insurance issuer that your practice is careless and will only result in another denial.

Your appeal letter and all supporting documentation are free from mistakes. Make sure that:

1.  Medical documentation is correct, appropriate, and relevant.
2.  Diagnoses (present and past) are accurate and supported using medical documentation.
3.  Healthcare Common Procedural Coding System (HCPCS) codes are accurate.
4.  An authorization approval number is included on the claim.
5.  Any modifiers for unique conditions or situations are accurate.

*   ### **Reconsideration**
    

Many clinics return the same claim with accompanying evidence and wait, hoping an insurance payer will reconsider. This is often a waste of time.

Instead, submit a formal appeal letter to the payor directly. It may take longer than just submitting the same or equivalent paperwork, but there are several advantages to this extra effort:

*   A resubmission of a claim will force insurers to investigate your claim more and address it faster than a simple resubmission.
*   Many insurance companies allow you to appeal through their official website. Use this service to file appeals as efficiently and securely as possible. You can also follow the progress of your appeal online.
*   A formal appeal letter indicates that you take the claim denial seriously. Drafting the letter will give your practice a better chance of thoroughly evaluating the claim and its accompanying documents.

It is important to submit a proper claim appeal letter on time. The sooner your claim is processed, the sooner you will get payment for the equipment provided. These will also put your patients at ease.

*   ### **Take a proactive approach.**
    

Being proactive when appealing DME claim denials entails more than simply ensuring that all of the coding is right and that the necessary signatures are there, although that is essential. There are reasons for claim denial, so preparing beforehand is a good idea.

#### **Learn about different claims processes.**

Each payor has a separate policy regarding the claims appeals procedure. Carriers may have varying claim filing methods, deadlines, and restrictions. That is why you should maintain an up-to-date running record of the claims appeal procedure for each payor. Take it a step further and write down why a claim got rejected so that future claims may be better understood.

#### **Open communication with the patient.**

You must maintain an open and honest conversation with your patients, so they understand the fees they may pay if their claim gets denied, how they may assist in the appeals process, and how they can answer any relevant questions to clear inaccuracies in your paperwork.

For example, suppose a patient’s record lacks sufficient information on their major complaints and previous treatments. During a visit, you may ask them about their previous treatments. They may connect you to a provider with the information you need to submit your appeal.
