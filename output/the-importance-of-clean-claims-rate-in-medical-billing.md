Submitting clean claims to payers is crucial to streamlining the revenue cycle. A complete and accurate claim is said to be a clean claim. When the payer accepts the claims on the first submission, it enhances the clean claims rate. Likewise, a provider receives swift payments due to clean claims submission. Some billing or coding inaccuracies may occur during the claim submission process. Therefore, **[outsourcing medical billing services](https://docsmedicalbilling.com/medical-billing-and-coding-services/)** can reduce the risk of errors.

Understanding Clean Claims
--------------------------

A clean claim is a medical bill submitted to payers with accurate information. The payer accepts a clean claim immediately and reimburses the provider. A clean claim rate is the cornerstone of an optimal revenue cycle, which increases by submitting clean claims to payers. Similarly, it streamlines the revenue cycle and protects the bottom line.

Characteristics of A Clean Claim
--------------------------------

A clean claim should have the following characteristics:

*   It should be complete with accurate information.
*   Attach accurate, required, and relevant documentation.
*   Write the accurate codes for the right services rendered.
*   Write down the pre-approvals and referrals if needed.

Importance of Clean Claims Rates
--------------------------------

The payer accepts the claim with accurate information and documentation, thus increasing the clean claims rates. Let’s break down its importance.

### Accurate and Timely Reimbursement

Clean claims reduce the risk of denial. When a provider submits a clean claim, the payer accepts it and sends payments accordingly. Furthermore, timely and accurate payments improve the revenue cycle.

### Reduced Administrative Expenses and Burden

When a payer rejects the claim, the healthcare staff has to resubmit it with accurate information. This increases the administrative tasks and burden of the hospital’s staff. Therefore, clean claims submission is crucial to reduce the administrative expenses and burden.

### Optimized Revenue

The overall revenue cycle depends on the reimbursement rate. An increase in reimbursements means higher revenue. Revenue leakages occur due to payment delays. In addition, the timely payments improve the financial performance.

Best Practices to Improve Clean Claims Rates
--------------------------------------------

Claim submission is not a one-step process. The billing staff fills the claim with relevant documentation and passes it to the claim scrub for approval. An accurate claim is sent to the payer for reimbursement, and an inaccurate claim is reversed for correction. So, let’s discuss the best practices to improve clean claims rates in detail:

### Accurate Patient/Provider Information

The first step is to provide accurate patient and provider information. Likewise, accurate information leads to claim acceptance. The billing staff must enter the patient’s address, name, and ID precisely in the claim.

### Accurate Coding

The second most important factor in the claims is the CPT, HCPCS, and ICD-10 codes. These codes identify the treatment type, like examination, diagnostics, surgical, multiple visits, X-rays, or multiple procedures. It is crucial to add the right codes for the right services rendered.

### Claim Scrubbing

Before submitting the claim directly to the payer, the provider sends it for claim scrubbing. The claim scrubber reviews the claim for possible billing and coding errors. Claim scrubbing increases the chances of swift claim acceptance through **[claim reviews](https://docsmedicalbilling.com/best-practices-for-effective-claims-reviews-in-medical-billing/)**, improving clean claims rates.

### Timely Claim Submission

When healthcare staff submit clean claims on time, it speeds up reimbursements. A delayed or late claim submission contributes to delayed payments from the payer. A healthcare biller must submit a clean claim promptly so that providers receive timely and immediate payments for the service rendered.

### Claim Denial Management

When the payer rejects the claim for payment, **[claim denial management](https://docsmedicalbilling.com/denied-claims-management-how-to-reduce-denials-effectively/)** helps review it. It involves thoroughly examining the claim. The claim reviews during claim denial management greatly help providers fix mistakes.
