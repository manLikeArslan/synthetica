# Mental Health Billing: Growing Importance of Behavioral Healthcare

*Original URL: https://www.medicalbillersandcoders.com/blog/importance-of-behavioral-healthcare/*

---

Mental health has long been a topic covered by misconceptions. However, in recent years, there has been a growing recognition of the significance of behavioral healthcare in improving the overall well-being of individuals. With this increased awareness, there has also been a surge in the demand for mental health services, which has brought the importance of **Behavioral Healthcare Billing** into the spotlight. This article explores the growing importance of behavioral healthcare and the critical role that proper mental health billing plays in supporting this crucial aspect of healthcare.

The most Common CPT Codes used for Behavioral Health Billing
------------------------------------------------------------

**Here are some of the most common CPT codes used for behavioral health billing:**

*   **90791** – Psychiatric diagnostic evaluation
*   **90832** – Psychotherapy, 30 minutes with patient and/or family member
*   **90834** – Psychotherapy, 45 minutes with patient and/or family member
*   **90837** – Psychotherapy, 60 minutes with patient and/or family member
*   **90839** – Psychotherapy for crisis; first 60 minutes
*   **90846** – Family psychotherapy (without the patient present)
*   **90847** – Family psychotherapy (with the patient present)
*   **90853** – Group psychotherapy (other than of a multiple-family group)
*   **96127** – Brief emotional/behavioral assessment (e.g., depression inventory, attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder \[ADHD\] scale)
*   **96152** – Individual psychophysiological therapy (e.g., biofeedback) with evaluation and reprogramming of the device

These codes are used to bill for various behavioral health services, including diagnostic evaluations, psychotherapy sessions, family therapy, group therapy, and psychophysiological therapy, among others. It’s essential to use the appropriate code based on the specific service provided during the patient encounter.

The Shift towards Behavioral Healthcare
---------------------------------------

Historically, mental health has often been sidelined or underfunded in healthcare systems around the world. However, a paradigm shift has been taking place, driven by a better understanding of the interplay between mental and physical health, as well as the recognition of the prevalence of mental health issues.

**Several factors have contributed to the growing importance of behavioral healthcare:**

*   **Prevalence of Mental Health Issues:** Mental health conditions affect a substantial portion of the global population. Conditions like depression, anxiety, and bipolar disorder can be as debilitating as many physical ailments, making it increasingly important to address them effectively.
*   **Integration with Physical Health:** The medical community has recognized that mental health is closely intertwined with physical health. For example, stress can exacerbate physical ailments, and mental health issues often co-occur with chronic diseases. As a result, addressing behavioral health is essential for comprehensive patient care.
*   **Changing Stigma:** Society has started to break down the stigma surrounding mental health, encouraging more individuals to seek help when needed. This cultural shift has led to increased demand for mental health services.
*   **Legislative Changes:** Legislation such as the Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act (MHPAEA) in the United States has mandated insurance providers to offer equal coverage for mental health and physical health treatments, making mental health services more accessible.

The Role of Mental Health Billing
---------------------------------

As behavioral healthcare gains prominence, mental health billing becomes crucial in ensuring that individuals have access to the services they need. Here’s why proper billing is so important:

*   **Financial Access:** Billing processes ensure that individuals can access mental health services without facing prohibitive financial barriers. Insurance claims and reimbursements make it more affordable for patients to seek help.
*   **Sustaining Providers:** Behavioral healthcare providers, including therapists, counselors, and psychiatrists, need proper reimbursement to sustain their practices. Accurate billing helps ensure that these professionals can continue offering essential services.
*   **Quality of Care:** Proper billing supports the quality of care provided by mental health professionals. When healthcare providers receive fair compensation, they can invest in training, resources, and infrastructure to deliver the best possible care to their patients.
*   **Compliance with Regulations:** Billing in the mental health sector is complex, with numerous regulations and codes that must be followed. Ensuring compliance not only avoids legal issues but also helps in maintaining transparency and accountability.
*   **Data for Improvement:** Accurate billing processes generate valuable data that can be used to analyze trends, identify gaps in service delivery, and improve the overall behavioral healthcare system.

Challenges in Mental Health Billing
-----------------------------------

**Despite its importance, mental health billing is not without its challenges:**

*   **Complex Coding:** [Behavioral healthcare billing](https://www.medicalbillersandcoders.com/blog/new-medicare-policies-for-behavioral-health-billing/) involves complex coding systems, such as the Current Procedural Terminology (CPT) and Healthcare Common Procedure Coding System (HCPCS), which can be challenging to navigate.
*   **Insurance Disputes:** Insurance providers may sometimes deny claims or under-reimburse providers, leading to disputes that can be time-consuming and financially draining.
*   **Regulatory Changes:** The healthcare industry is subject to regulatory changes, and mental health billing must adapt to evolving requirements.
*   **Data Privacy Concerns:** Behavioral health data is sensitive, and providers must navigate strict data privacy regulations, such as the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act ([HIPAA](https://www.cms.gov/priorities/key-initiatives/burden-reduction/administrative-simplification/hipaa/privacy-security-information)).

Tips for Successful Mental Health Billing
-----------------------------------------

**There are several things that mental health providers can do to ensure successful billing.**

*   **First**, it is important to have a good understanding of the billing requirements for all of the payer sources that you accept. This information can be found on the payer websites or by contacting the payer directly.
*   **Second**, it is important to have a well-documented billing system. This means keeping accurate records of all of the services that you provide to your patients. It is also important to have a system in place for submitting claims to insurance companies and government programs.
*   **Finally**, it is important to be aware of the latest changes to mental health billing requirements. Insurance companies and government programs frequently update their billing requirements, so it is important to stay informed of these changes.

Behavioral healthcare is gaining the recognition it deserves, with growing awareness of its importance in overall well-being. Mental health billing plays a pivotal role in supporting this shift by ensuring financial access to services, sustaining providers, maintaining quality of care, and promoting compliance with regulations.

As the demand for mental health services continues to rise, healthcare systems and providers must prioritize proper billing processes, ultimately contributing to improved mental health outcomes for individuals and communities.
