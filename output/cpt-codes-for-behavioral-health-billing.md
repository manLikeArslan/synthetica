**Description**

Initial psychiatric diagnostic evaluation without medical services

90832

Psychotherapy, 30 minutes with patient and/or family member.

90834

Psychotherapy, 45 minutes with patient and/or family member.

90837

Psychotherapy, 60 minutes with patient and/or family member.

90847

Family psychotherapy (conjoint psychotherapy) with the patient present.

90853

FAQs
----

**1\. What are CPT codes for Behavioral Health Billing?**

CPT codes are a set of numbers used to identify specific medical procedures and services. In behavioral health billing, CPT codes are used to describe the services provided during a therapy session or psychiatric evaluation.

**2\. Why are CPT Codes important in Behavioral Health Billing?**

CPT codes help healthcare providers and insurance companies understand the type of services provided during a behavioral health session. They also determine how much the insurance company will reimburse the provider for those services.

**3\. What is the CPT code for behavioral screening?**

The CPT code for behavioral screening is 96127. This code is used for screening for depression, anxiety, and other behavioral conditions.

**4\. What are some Common CPT Codes used in Behavioral Health Billing?**

Common CPT codes in Behavioral Health Billing include:  
1\. 90834: Individual psychotherapy session, typically lasting 45-50 minutes.  
2\. 90837: Individual psychotherapy session, typically lasting 60 minutes or longer.  
3\. 90791: Initial psychiatric evaluation, without medical services.  
4\. 90847: Family psychotherapy (with the patient present).

**5\. How do I know which CPT Code to use for a specific service?**

The appropriate CPT code depends on the type and length of the service provided. Healthcare providers should carefully document the services rendered during each session to ensure accurate billing.
