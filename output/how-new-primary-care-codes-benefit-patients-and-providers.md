FAQs
----

**1\. What is the most common procedure code in primary care?**

The most commonly used procedure code in primary care is a tie between 99213 and 99214, both representing routine office visits for established patients.

**2\. What is the most common method of payment for physicians?**

The most common method of payment for physicians depends on factors like region, specialty, and employment model, but generally falls within fee-for-service, salary, or a mix of both.

**3\. How would a primary care practice be paid under Patient-Centered Primary Care Payment?**

A primary care practice under Patient-Centered Payment receives monthly fees for wellness, chronic condition management, and acute care, plus per-visit fees for acute episodes, replacing the complex web of fees in the traditional system.

**4\. Would Patient-Centered Primary Care Payment help reduce the shortage of primary care physicians?**

Patient-centered payment has the potential to reduce the primary care shortage by improving compensation, boosting physician satisfaction, and encouraging medical students to choose the field, but its long-term impact remains uncertain.

**5\. How does Patient-Centered Primary Care Payment differ from Direct Primary Care?**

Patient-centered payment uses capitated fees within the existing insurance system, while Direct Primary Care operates outside insurance with flat, monthly patient fees.

**6\. Why wouldn’t health insurance plans want to implement Patient-Centered Primary Care Payment?**

Health insurance plans might hesitate over [Patient-Centered Payment](https://chqpr.org/Patient-Centered_Payment.html) due to upfront cost increases, concerns about reduced profits, and the possibility of free-riding benefits from other payers.
