What is Ambulatory Surgery Center Billing?
------------------------------------------

Outpatient surgical units that allow providers to perform surgeries and deliver medical care are called ambulatory surgery centers (ASCs). Likewise, the process of converting these services into compliant codes for providers to receive accurate reimbursement is called ambulatory surgery center billing.  
ASC billing differs from traditional hospital billing. It involves same-day, non-emergency procedures performed in an outpatient setting where the patient recovers without inpatient admission. Proper use of the Place of Service (POS) code 24 is essential to identify ASC services to payers.

Top 5 Challenges in Ambulatory Surgery Center Billing
-----------------------------------------------------

### Complex Coding and Documentation

*   ASC procedures are complex for coding. When it comes to ambulatory surgery center billing, missing or wrong ASC codes are one of the challenges providers experience.
*   CPT, ICD-10, and HCPCS hold significance in medical billing. Using them inaccurately results in claim rejections.
*   Obsolete documentation means the claim lacks the necessary information that proves the medical necessity of ASC services. Likewise, payers reject the claim with incomplete documentation.

### Payment Delays and Denials

*   Payment delays are major concerns for providers because they hurt the overall revenue cycle.
*   It occurs due to a claim lacking information necessary for payers to acknowledge the necessity of the ASC services.
*   It can also occur due to inadequate eligibility verification. Payer denies payment for the services that are not part of the patient’s insurance plan.
*   Similarly, some procedures require pre-treatment approvals to ensure the payer will cover the treatment charges. Failing to obtain prior authorization approvals often results in payment denials.

### Regulatory Compliance and Reimbursement Changes

*   Ambulatory surgery center billing requirements change with changes in CMS and private payers’ guidelines.  
    Failing to keep yourself up to date with billing guidelines and requirements results in a compliance breach.
*   Providers must track changes to the Outpatient Prospective Payment System (OPPS) and the CMS ASC Covered Procedures List to ensure compliant claim submission and reimbursement.
*   Furthermore, the reimbursement rate can also change with time. Submitting a claim on old payment rates causes denials and financial strain.

### Patient Billing and Collection

*   Out-of-pocket medical billing is the payment that a patient needs to pay before and after the treatment.
*   Collecting payment upfronts and outstanding dues from patients is another challenge you’ll experience as a provider.
*   Aging accounts can be challenging to recover, which will hurt the financial performance of your medical practice.

### Billing System Integration with Clinical Workflow

*   Another challenge is the lack of interoperability between electronic health records (EHR) and billing software.
*   It increases the data entry errors, which results in billing and reimbursement delays.
