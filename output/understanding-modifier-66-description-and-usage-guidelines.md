# Understanding Modifier 66: Description & Usage Guidelines

*Original URL: https://docsmedicalbilling.com/understanding-modifier-66-description-usage-guidelines/*

---

Accurate procedural coding and modifier usage are vital to clean claims and proper reimbursement. Modifier 66 is a specialized modifier used for surgical team billing, allowing multiple surgeons from different specialties to be reimbursed for performing distinct parts of the same complex surgery. This article explains appropriate use, key examples, and compliance guidelines based on CMS and payer standards.

It is appended to a CPT code when a surgical team, generally consisting of three or more surgeons, often with **[different specialties](https://docsmedicalbilling.com/most-common-general-surgery-cpt-codes/)**, works together during a complex surgical procedure. Each team member performs a clearly defined portion of the surgery, and the modifier indicates this collaborative effort on the claim.  
This modifier allows each surgeon to bill the procedure code with the modifier appended, reflecting their participation in the case.

Modifier 66 is used for surgical teams of three or more surgeons. For surgeries involving only two surgeons, the appropriate modifier is Modifier 62 (Co-surgeon).

Typically, surgeons on a team have different specialties or skills necessary for the surgery. For instance, an organ transplant might involve a transplant surgeon, a vascular surgeon, and a general surgeon.

It applies only to surgical procedures designated by payers as eligible for team surgery billing. It is not used for non-surgical services like radiation treatment management.

Modifier 66 plays a crucial role in correctly indicating team surgical involvement on claims where three or more surgeons collaborate. Accurate, clear documentation combined with correct modifier usage ensures the surgical team receives rightful reimbursement and maintains compliance with CMS and payer policies. Avoid common pitfalls such as under-documentation and misuse of the modifier to streamline your billing process and reduce claim denials.
