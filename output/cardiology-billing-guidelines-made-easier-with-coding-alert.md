**FAQs**
--------

**1\. How do the new cardiology billing guidelines impact reimbursement rates?**

CMS has reduced reimbursement rates for imaging services, with a 22% cut to the technical modules of lower-priced services, making accurate coding even more critical.

**2\. Why is documentation so important in cardiology billing?**

Accurate documentation ensures claims are processed quickly, reduces the chance of errors, and speeds up reimbursements, especially in the face of reduced reimbursements and heightened scrutiny.

**3\. How can coding errors affect cardiology practices?**

Coding mistakes can lead to claim denials, delayed payments, and audits, which can result in lost revenue and added administrative work for your practice.

**4\. What are add-on codes and why do they matter?**

Add-on codes are used alongside primary [CPT codes](https://www.cms.gov/medicare/regulations-guidance/physician-self-referral/list-cpt-hcpcs-codes) for additional procedures. Properly pairing them is crucial to avoid coding errors and ensure accurate billing, especially with pacemaker revisions and complex procedures.
