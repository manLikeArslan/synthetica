Common Causes of Claim Rejections:

1.  Incorrect Patient Details

Claim rejections continue to impact healthcare providers by slowing revenue cycles and increasing administrative workload. While some issues are due to payer-specific rules, most rejections result from preventable mistakes such as incorrect patient data, invalid insurance details, or coding inconsistencies.

### Cause 1: Incorrect Patient Details

Errors in spelling, birthdates, or policy numbers often result in claims being rejected immediately at the clearinghouse level. Even minor discrepancies can cause a mismatch with payer databases.

#### Solution:

MBC uses real-time verification systems to ensure patient demographics are accurate before submission. By validating insurance and identity details early, providers avoid common patient information errors in medical billing.

### Cause 2: Invalid Insurance Information

Claims sent to the wrong payer or tied to expired insurance plans are quickly rejected. These issues typically occur when insurance changes go unrecorded or coverage is not confirmed during service.

#### Solution:

As a [leading medical billing and coding service provider](https://www.medicalbillersandcoders.com/), MBC verifies coverage before each visit, confirming eligibility, correct payer ID, and benefits coordination. This workflow helps prevent claim rejection due to insurance verification errors.

### Cause 3: Incorrect Coding and Modifier Usage

Medical coding errors remain one of the most frequent causes of rejected claims. These include incorrect CPT or [ICD-10 codes](https://www.cms.gov/medicare/coding-billing/icd-10-codes), outdated code sets, or invalid modifier combinations.

#### Solution:

MBC’s certified coders stay current with coding changes and payer-specific rules. We also use intelligent scrubbing tools to detect medical coding errors that cause claim rejections before the claim is submitted.

### Cause 4: Missing Required Documentation

Some procedures require [prior authorization](https://www.medicalbillersandcoders.com/blog/prior-authorization-in-medical-billing/), referral letters, or detailed clinical notes. Submitting claims without these supporting documents often results in rejection.

#### Solution:

MBC maintains payer-specific documentation checklists and integrates directly with EHR systems to ensure every required document is included at submission. This helps prevent medical claim rejection due to missing documentation.

### Cause 5: Filing Claims After Deadlines

Each payer enforces a time limit for filing claims, ranging from 30 to 365 days. Submissions outside this window are often rejected outright and are non-recoverable.

#### Solution:

MBC tracks filing deadlines by payer and service date using automated tools. Prioritizing claims approaching their filing limits helps clients avoid timely filing denials in medical billing.

### Cause 6: Provider Credentialing and NPI Mismatches

Claims are typically rejected if a provider is not properly credentialed with the payer or the submitted NPI does not match the payer’s records.

#### Solution:

MBC offers full [credentialing services](https://www.medicalbillersandcoders.com/revenue-management-services.aspx?DivId=physician-credentialing) and enrolment support. All provider information—including NPIs, taxonomy codes, and payer enrolments —is kept current to reduce claim rejections due to NPI and credentialing issues.

### Cause 7: Duplicate Submissions

Accidental duplicate submissions occur when the claim status is unclear or systems lack tracking capabilities. Payers reject duplicates even if the original claim is still under review.

#### Solution:

MBC provides a centralized claims dashboard that tracks submission and payment statuses in real-time. This level of oversight prevents unnecessary resubmissions and teaches teams how to prevent duplicate claims in healthcare billing.

### Cause 8: Incomplete Service-Level Data

Missing or incorrect entries for service dates, units billed, or place of service codes can result in claim rejections, especially with strict commercial payers.

#### Solution:

Pre-submission checks built into MBC’s **claim management** system ensure all service-level fields are completed accurately. This reduces the chances of claim rejection due to missing service details in billing.

****2\. What is the difference between a claim rejection and a claim denial?****

A **claim rejection** occurs when a payer refuses to accept a claim due to formatting, coding, or data entry errors—before it enters the adjudication process. A **denial** happens after a claim has been reviewed but is refused payment due to coverage or policy rules.  
MBC addresses both, but emphasizes **preventing rejections at the source** through clean submissions.

****4\. How do No Surprises Act rules affect medical claim submissions?****

The No Surprises Act requires more transparent billing and specific disclosures for out-of-network services. Claims must now include:  
1\. Accurate provider status  
2\. Advanced cost estimates for self-pay patients  
3\. Documentation of consent (when applicable)
