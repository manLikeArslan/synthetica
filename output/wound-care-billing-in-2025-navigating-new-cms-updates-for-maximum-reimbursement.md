Key CMS 2025 Updates for Wound Care Billing
-------------------------------------------

### 1\. Updated Payment Rates for Wound Care Procedures

CMS has revised reimbursement rates for debridement, skin substitute grafts, and negative pressure wound therapy (NPWT).

*   Debridement codes (CPT 11042-11047) see a 2.5% increase in Medicare reimbursement.
*   Skin substitute grafts now require enhanced documentation for prior authorization.
*   CMS introduces stricter utilization review for hyperbaric oxygen therapy (HBOT).

### 2\. Prior Authorization Expansions

In 2025, CMS expanded prior authorization requirements for high-cost wound care treatments, including:

*   Skin substitute grafts (CPT 15271-15278)
*   Negative pressure wound therapy (CPT 97605-97606)
*   Hyperbaric oxygen therapy (CPT 99183)

MBC Strategy: We conduct real-time eligibility verification and handle prior authorization submissions to prevent reimbursement delays.

### 3\. New Documentation Requirements for Skin Substitute Grafts

CMS now requires more detailed clinical evidence for graft applications, including:

*   Photographic wound progression records
*   Previous treatment failures before grafting
*   Medical necessity justification for repeated applications

### 4\. Modifier Changes for Bundled Payments

*   Modifier 25 usage tightened for E/M services billed on the same day as a procedure.
*   New restrictions on Modifier 59 for separately billable wound care procedures.

### 3\. Negative Pressure Wound Therapy (NPWT)

NPWT is reimbursed only with supporting medical necessity documentation.

*   CPT 97605 (Wound ≤50 cm²)
*   CPT 97606 (Wound >50 cm²)
