What Are SNF Billing Guidelines?
--------------------------------

SNF billing guidelines are a set of rules defined by Medicare and private payers. They ensure Skilled Nursing Facilities bill properly for covered services. These rules cover:

*   **Admission and eligibility checks**
*   **PDPM coding and classification**
*   **Billing timelines and form requirements**
*   **Proper use of SNF benefit periods**
*   **Documentation standards for audits**

Each step must be followed closely to avoid claim denials or repayment demands.

2025 SNF Billing Highlights You Can’t Ignore
--------------------------------------------

Here are the top billing updates impacting SNFs in 2025:

### 1\. **PDPM Accuracy Is Still Under the Microscope**

The **Patient-Driven Payment Model (PDPM)** has shifted focus to clinical needs, not therapy minutes. But CMS audits are ramping up in 2025.

SNFs must ensure MDS assessments are accurate. Diagnosis coding must match documentation exactly.

### 2\. **Timely Billing Is Critical**

Claims must be submitted within **one year** of the service date. Delayed claims—even by one day—can be rejected.

Timely submission also helps manage AR days and improves cash flow.

### 3\. **Benefit Period Management**

SNFs must track benefit periods and days used. Medicare Part A covers up to **100 days per benefit period**, with strict restart rules.

Improper tracking leads to billing non-covered services to Medicare.

### 4\. **Consolidated Billing Still Applies**

Under [**SNF Consolidated Billing**](https://www.medicalbillersandcoders.com/blog/skilled-nursing-facility-snf-consolidated-billing-cb/), most services must be billed by the SNF—even if provided by outside vendors.

Mistakes here often lead to coordination of benefits errors and compliance risks.

Common Pitfalls in SNF Billing
------------------------------

Even experienced teams run into trouble. Here are key mistakes to watch for:

*   Incomplete or outdated MDS assessments
*   Incorrect HIPPS codes
*   Missing therapy documentation
*   Billing Part B services during Part A stays
*   Lack of supporting physician documentation

Each of these can cause delayed or denied payments.

FAQs About SNF Billing Guidelines
---------------------------------

****What does SNF stand for in billing?****

SNF stands for Skilled Nursing Facility. These are facilities that provide 24/7 nursing care and rehabilitation services.

****What is PDPM in SNF billing?****

PDPM (Patient-Driven Payment Model) is Medicare’s system for reimbursing SNFs based on clinical characteristics, not therapy time.

****What is SNF consolidated billing?****

Under consolidated billing, SNFs must include most services on one bill—even if provided by an outside entity.

****How many days does Medicare cover in an SNF?****

[Medicare Part A](https://www.medicare.gov/providers-services/original-medicare/part-a) covers up to 100 days per benefit period, with full coverage for the first 20 days and partial coverage after that.

****Why do SNF claims get denied?****

Common denial reasons include missing documentation, incorrect coding, untimely submission, and billing outside the benefit period.
