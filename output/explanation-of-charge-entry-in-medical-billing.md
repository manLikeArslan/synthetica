Similarly, the process of compiling and recording medical information to enter into the billing system is called charge entry in medical billing. What is Charge Entry in Medical Billing?
----------------------------------------

It is the process of entering medical data, such as codes, dates of services, charges, patient demographics, and provider information, into the billing system to generate claims and receive payment from the payers. It includes gathering data on patients’ demographics, verifying insurance, and processing payment methods to ensure accurate entry of charges. Accurate charge entry enables providers to submit clean claims to the payer. Similarly, it ensures providers receive timely payments for the services they render.  
**Note:** Charge entry occurs after medical coding when the assigned ICD-10, CPT, and HCPCS codes are inputted into the system along with related service details to prepare claims.

Importance of Charge Entry
--------------------------

### Coding Accuracy

Charge entry in medical billing includes converting services into codes to streamline the billing operation. Therefore, accurately entering ICD-10, CPT, and HCPCS codes into the billing system enhances the reimbursement process. It further ensures providers receive timely payment, streamlining the financial health.

### Reduced Claim Denials

When the billing staff accurately enters or captures charges into the system before claim submission, it reduces the risk of denials. Thus, seamless data entry ensures clean claim submission to payers, resulting in reduced claim denials.

### Improved Revenue Efficiency

Documentation and coding errors in charge entry can result in incorrect charges. Clean claim submissions, resulting from accurate charge entry, speed up the billing process. Thus, you can easily improve RCM efficiency by accurately entering charges into the billing system.

Key Components of Charge Entry
------------------------------

### Patient Demographics

Registering patients to receive medical services from the providers. Likewise, charge entry in medical billing involves collecting patient information, such as name, ID, age, address, and other relevant details. The information helps the administrative staff schedule an appointment.

### Eligibility Verification

It includes verifying the patient’s insurance plan before receiving treatment to ensure they are eligible for insurance coverage. Hence, timely and accurate insurance verification prevents claim rejections.

### Coding and Modifiers

Converting codes into charges enables physicians to receive reimbursement for the services they provide to patients. Billing staff must adhere to CMS and AMA coding guidelines. Hence, it is crucial to enter correct codes and modifiers for the services to avoid billing discrepancies.

### Charge Posting and Entry

After assigning codes to services, the next step is to determine payments for the charges. It includes the payer contract, negotiated and discounted amounts, and the payment methods. Afterwards, the decided amounts are entered into the billing system to receive timely reimbursement.

Common Charge Entry Challenges and Solutions
--------------------------------------------

### Data Entry Errors

Data entry errors, such as incorrect charge entry, wrong procedure codes, inaccurate procedure codes, result in claim denials. It occurs when the billing staff makes an error and doesn’t verify the information while capturing charges. Hence, minimize errors by validating tools and information before entering charges. Incorrect charge entry can lead to claim denials and payment delays. Incorrect code assignment to services also causes data entry errors. While submitting data and entering charges, maintaining accuracy is necessary.

### Obsolete Documentation

It is difficult to enter charges without complete documentation and accurate codes. Hence, the right documentation as per the payer’s requirements accelerates the charge entry process. Therefore, ensure to collect complete documentation before entering charges into the billing system.

### Compliance Breach

Failing to adhere to the payer’s guidelines, laws, policies, and ethical standards results in a compliance breach. Therefore, achieve consistent cash flow by adhering to the billing and coding regulations.
