#### Optimization and Examination of Account Receivable (A/R)

For physicians account receivable is one of the important elements as far as reimbursements are concerned. Moreover, they advised keeping your A/R between 0 to 30 days category. In A/R, days indicate the average time taken to collect the payments owing to practice. Generally, days and payments are directly proportional to each other. More the number of days, delays in obtaining payment. Calculating A/R will help you to find out whether the revenue management cycle processes of your practice are effective as well as efficient or not and also point out any problems that occurred in the time-to-time payments.

**The following are some methods for how to optimize and examine account receivable (A/R), which will help you to have a positive impact on your revenue cycle:**

• Differentiate patient accounts receivable and insurance  
• Maintaining relationships with payers  
• Evaluate and understand payer-refund patterns to define, determine, and prioritize risks  
• Understanding particular guidelines by payers that could affect your account receivable management  
• Enforce stringent collection policies for patient accounts

FAQs
----

**1\. Why is Account Receivable (A/R) important for chiropractic billing?**

A/R represents the time taken to collect payments, directly impacting cash flow. Keeping A/R within 0-30 days ensures quicker reimbursement and a more efficient revenue cycle.

**2\. How can I optimize and examine my practice’s A/R?**

To optimize A/R, differentiate between patient and insurance accounts, build strong relationships with payers, and enforce strict collection policies for patient accounts. Regularly evaluate payer-refund patterns to reduce risks.

**3\. Why is proper documentation crucial for chiropractic billing?**

Proper documentation ensures accurate coding and proof of medical necessity, reducing errors that could lead to claim denials. Insufficient or incorrect documentation is a common reason for improper payments in chiropractic services.

**4\. How can I educate my patients about chiropractic billing and insurance?**

Provide patients with clear information about their insurance coverage, co-pays, and deductibles. Offering educational materials like booklets can help them understand their financial responsibilities and the cost of care.

**5\. Why should I review my insurance contracts annually?**

Reviewing insurance contracts helps identify changes in coverage or reimbursement rates and allows you to adjust your workflows or staff processes accordingly. It also helps ensure you meet filing deadlines and stay compliant.
