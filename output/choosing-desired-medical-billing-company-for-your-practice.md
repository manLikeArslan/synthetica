**Evaluate Your Practice’s Pain Points**
----------------------------------------

As you are seeking external help in [**Medical Billing and Coding**](https://www.medicalbillersandcoders.com/medical-billing-services.aspx) for your practice, there are some pain points that are badly affecting your practice’s revenue. FAQs
----

**1\. How can I evaluate if a medical billing company can address my practice’s pain points?**

Start by listing your practice’s key challenges, such as hiring skilled staff, claim rejections, and poor reimbursements. Then, communicate these challenges to the billing companies you are considering to ensure they can offer solutions.

**2\. What is the ideal claim submission timeline for a medical billing company?**

Ideally, claims should be submitted within 24 hours of a patient visit. This ensures quicker reimbursements and improves your practice’s cash flow.

**3\. What is a clean claim, and why is it important?**

A clean claim is a claim that has accurate and complete information, leading to fewer rejections. The industry standard for clean claim submission is 90%, but [**Medical Billers and Coders (MBC)**](https://www.medicalbillersandcoders.com/) achieve over 95%.

**4\. How should a medical billing company handle rejected claims?**

A [good medical billing company](https://www.medicalbillersandcoders.com/) cross-checks patient and insurance information against the payer database before submission, helping minimize rejections. They should also have a process in place to quickly resolve and resubmit rejected claims.

**5\. What kind of reports should I expect from my medical billing company?**

Expect customized reports such as accounts receivable (AR) aging, payment trends, insurance and patient collections, KPI indicators, payer analysis, and rejection reports. These reports help you track the financial health of your practice.
