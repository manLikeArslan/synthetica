**Here are some trends to watch out for:**
------------------------------------------

### **Payers will continue to look at ways to cut costs**

Insurers have created healthcare policies, designed plans, and tightened provider networks to cut down healthcare costs and they have shown success in 2018. As a professional medical billing and RCM expert, our advice is to expect payers to accelerate those programs and policies and search for more cost-cutting techniques in 2020.

For hospitals and healthcare facilities looking for ways to improve their bottom line, many clinicians viewed imaging as a way to make up for lost dollars and elsewhere. However, experts also say that the payer’s message was that medical necessity is the stronger consideration and that unit pricing needs to reflect broader market pricing.

Many providers had earlier turned this under-regulated service line into a profit center, but regulatory actions made it clear that such activities would not be acceptable.

### **Organizations will continue to improve denial and reimbursement settlements**

Claim denials and documentation to support reimbursement appeals are the core regions where the revenue cycle management continues to struggle. Small, large, solo, or Ambulatory; all are seeking innovative ways to improve the RCM processes and reduce denial rates, which is via third-party systems, or (if possible) within the host system.

### **Training and adoption for the new systems**

As the healthcare industry transits from a legacy to a new and modern system, what it needs to consider is how to handle the change of process management for their staff. Some offices have been using modern systems for more than 10 years now. As proper training and adoption given by the employer are what propelled this successful transformation, expect the same in 2020.

**FAQs**
--------

### 1\. What is the revenue cycle management process in healthcare?

[Revenue cycle management (RCM)](https://www.medicalbillersandcoders.com/revenue-management-services.aspx) is the financial process healthcare facilities use, from scheduling appointments to receiving payment. It includes billing, coding, and reimbursement activities that ensure payments are collected efficiently.

### 2\. How are healthcare payers trying to cut costs in 2020?

Payers are tightening provider networks, adjusting policies, and focusing on cost-control techniques. This includes emphasizing the medical necessity of services over previously profitable areas like imaging.

### 3\. Why do hospitals continue to struggle with claim denials and reimbursement settlements?

Denial management and documentation for reimbursement appeals remain challenging for many healthcare providers. Innovations in third-party systems and internal processes are essential for reducing these issues.

### 4\. Why is training and system adoption important for revenue cycle management?

Transitioning from legacy systems to modern ones requires proper staff training to ensure smooth adoption. In 2020, healthcare facilities must focus on providing staff with the tools and knowledge for effective system implementation.

### 5\. How is cost transparency impacting healthcare in 2020?

Patients are increasingly demanding transparency regarding healthcare costs and reimbursement rates. This shift is pushing healthcare providers to offer clearer pricing information and collaborate more with insurers to meet patient expectations.
