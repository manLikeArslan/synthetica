Payment delays disrupt the revenue cycle and create unnecessary stress for healthcare providers, hindering their focus on patient care. The primary reason behind healthcare reimbursement challenges is claim denials. Several reasons lead to denied medical claims. According to statistics, 65% of denied claims are never reworked. Therefore, denied claims management can reduce the chances of leaving money on the table for healthcare providers. In the same way, medical practices can stay profitable in the competitive healthcare marketplace.  
Implementing the proper denial management solutions can enable physicians to decrease the number of claim denials. As a result, it leads to **[revenue cycle optimization](https://docsmedicalbilling.com/healthcare-revenue-cycle-management-services/)**, and providers can get paid for all the medical services. Much effort is required to manage denied insurance claims of medical practices. Let’s discuss the primary reasons behind claim denials.

Top 5 Reasons Behind Claim Denials
----------------------------------

### Prior Authorization

One of the biggest reasons for medical billing denials is prior authorization. The best practice is to obtain prior authorization before the patient’s visit or performing the procedure. Prior authorization plays a key role in streamlining denied claims management.

### Patient Insurance Coverage

Offering medical services that patient insurance does not cover is another primary reason for claim denials. Medical practices should also create a system verifying patients’ eligibility before offering services. Likewise, providing medically unnecessary services causes denials.

### Incomplete Information

To avoid the insurance claim appeal process and ensure clean claim submission, the billing staff should submit claims with complete information. For instance, patient details, insurance details, and coding information must be correct to avoid denials.

### Medical Coding Errors

Accurate CPT and ICD-10 codes are mandatory to create error-free medical claims. Similarly, medical coders should use correct modifiers to avoid claim denials. Additionally, managing the services that can be bundled or billed separately is essential to prevent payment delays.

### Claim Filing Delays

To achieve optimal denied claims management, medical practices must file the claim within the time limit. The insurance payer sets the timely filing limit (TFL), and providers must follow it to avoid claim denials.

How Providers Can Optimize Denied Claims Management
---------------------------------------------------

Here are some actionable steps that can help providers reduce denials and improve reimbursements from payers:

### Analyze Denial Trends

Identifying the most common reasons behind claim denials allows providers to analyze recurring issues in billing and coding, allowing medical practices to address these issues.

### Proactive Eligibility Verification

Medical practices should check patients’ eligibility before rendering medical services to minimize the chances of denied claims. It saves both the time and resources of healthcare organizations.

### Staff Training

The coders and billers at a medical practice should be trained to use accurate CPT codes, perform prior authorization, and check patient insurance eligibility.

### Outsource Denied Claims Management

Healthcare providers can hire professional medical billing companies that offer denial management services, reducing the hassle of managing denied claims.

### Utilize Technology Solutions

Using software solutions to automate the process of using accurate codes, checking eligibility, and correcting patient information reduces manual errors

### Claim Scrubbing

Utilize advanced solutions for claim scrubbing. It can check the claim before submitting it to the insurance payer. Technology solutions can minimize the mistakes as manual scrubbing is prone to errors.

### Submit Claim on Time

Claim denial resolution takes time and resources. Thus, it is better to follow the timely filing limit set by the insurance payer to avoid denials.
