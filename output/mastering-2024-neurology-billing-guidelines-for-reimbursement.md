Assessment, Handling, and Medical Decision Making
-------------------------------------------------

#### There are two methods to assist neurologists in assessing the quality of treatment they have delivered:

1.  Medical Decision Making (MDM): MDM focuses on the complexity of the decision-making process.
2.  Evaluation and Management (E/M): E/M coding considers the scope of the patient visit’s history, inspection, and medical decision-making.

Both MDM AND E/M calculate the proper amount of compensation, considering the complexity and volume of labor involved, for the services rendered by neurologists. E/M services have five service levels, ranging from Level 1 (minimal) to Level 5 (complex), based on the level of complexity of the services provided. Four MDM types are comparable to this: simple, low, moderate, and high complexity.

Following the establishment of these levels, billers translate each technique or service into industry-accepted alphanumeric codes. Healthcare practitioners primarily use two coding systems: the International Classification of Diseases, Tenth Revision (ICD-10) and Current Procedural Terminology (CPT).

### Examples of Neurology ICD and CPT Codes

ICD-10 codes for nervous-system illnesses vary from G00 to G99. Here are a few instances. Consider how a single-digit variation might substantially alter the diagnosis.

*   Alzheimer’s disease with early-onset G30.0
*   Alzheimer’s disease with late-onset G30.1
*   Other Alzheimer’s diseases G30.8
*   Sleep apnea, unspecified G47.30
*   Primary central sleep apnea (G47.31)

The CPT codes for neurology and neuromuscular operations are 95700–96020. Here are some examples of categories:

*   Electromyography procedures: 95860–95872
*   Routine EEG procedures: 95812–95836
*   Nerve conduction tests: 95905–95913

Neurology Revenue Cycle Management Steps
----------------------------------------

### **Step 1: Insurance Eligibility Verification and Pre-Authorization.**

Before providing any treatments, it is critical to determine the patient’s insurance coverage and eligibility. Neurological surgery coverage varies and is often contingent upon the patient’s insurance coverage. This evaluation helps patients discover prospective out-of-pocket expenditures and estimate their financial commitments. Precise confirmation helps to ensure timely payment for supplied services and reduces the likelihood of claim rejections.

### **Step 2: Providing Services in Neurology and Receiving Payments**

The second part consists of providing services and collecting money. Healthcare practitioners must rigorously document all elements of the patient interaction, including the length of the visit, any diagnostic tests or treatments performed, and the decision-making strategy used. These specifications, known as the MDM (Medical Decision Making) and E/M (Evaluation and Management) levels, are then used to validate the services offered. This billing process guarantees correct billing to the patient’s insurance company.

### **Step 3: Reporting Neurology Claims**

Professional coders translate patient data into CPT and ICD-10 codes. Neurology billers use these codes to create payment claims and send them to the patient’s insurance company.

### **Step 4: Payment**

In the payment of neurology revenue cycle management, insurance companies review and process submitted claims. Approved claims receive payment, while denied claims require either an appeal or resubmission.

Additionally, billers send invoices to patients for any remaining balances after insurance payouts. They also follow up on late or outstanding payments, requiring familiarity with insurance plans’ explanations of benefits to clarify charges to patients.

### **Step 5: Reporting Data**

Data reporting is crucial to medical billing in neurology because it enables practices to monitor their financial situation. Reports must be made to learn more about the practice’s financial position. Frequent examination of these reports enhances decision-making and identifies opportunities for improvement. Regulatory bodies may also demand data reporting to confirm treatment compliance and quality.
