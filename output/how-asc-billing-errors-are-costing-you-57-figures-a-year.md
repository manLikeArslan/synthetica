**Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)**
-------------------------------------

****1\. What is the average denial rate for Ambulatory Surgery Centers (ASCs)?****

The average claim denial rate for ASCs ranges between **15% and 20%**, according to industry and CMS data. Denials commonly stem from modifier misuse, missing prior authorizations, and documentation gaps. Without proactive denial management, these rejected claims often turn into permanent revenue loss rather than recoverable AR.

****2\. Why are modifiers such as -59, -25, and -RT/-LT critical in ASC billing?****

Modifiers communicate essential details about how and why procedures were performed. In ASC billing, incorrect modifier usage frequently triggers **automatic payer denials** or underpayments. Since ASCs rely heavily on multiple-procedure billing, proper modifier application is crucial to avoid bundling errors and ensure full reimbursement.

****3\. How does prior authorization impact ASC revenue?****

Prior authorization failures account for approximately **35% of ASC claim denials**. Each denied surgical case can result in an average loss of **$2,000–$3,000**. Effective authorization tracking and verification before the date of service is one of the fastest ways to reduce denial rates and protect revenue.

****4\. How long should ASCs wait before pursuing old AR recovery?****

ASCs should begin aggressive AR recovery once claims reach **90–120 days** unpaid. Many billing companies abandon aged accounts, but these claims are still recoverable through appeals, payer follow-ups, and compliance-driven negotiations. Structured AR recovery can unlock **hundreds of thousands of dollars** in previously written-off revenue.

****5\. How do government regulations affect ASC billing compliance?****

ASC billing is governed by CMS regulations, Medicare payment systems, and national correct coding initiatives (NCCI). Non-compliance—whether intentional or accidental—can lead to audits, payment recoupments, or penalties. Staying aligned with CMS billing rules and coding edits is essential for both revenue protection and legal compliance.
