What is TherapyNotes and How Does its Billing Work?
---------------------------------------------------

TherapyNotes is a behavioral-health-specific EHR and practice management system that connects scheduling, clinical documentation, patient portal, telehealth, and billing in one platform. Its billing module is fully integrated with notes and appointments so that once documentation is completed, charges can be generated and queued for claim submission with minimal extra work.

Key TherapyNotes billing capabilities typically include:

*   Electronic claim submission (EDI) to a wide network of payers
*   Integrated claim workflows tied to completed notes and appointments
*   ERA (Electronic Remittance Advice) posting to simplify payment posting
*   Patient statements, superbills, and balance tracking
*   Integrated card-on-file payment processing for copays and self-pay balances
*   Billing dashboards and reports to monitor charges, payments, and A/R

  
When configured correctly, the system can move you from session scheduling all the way through claim submission, posting, and patient collections inside a single environment.

Setting Up TherapyNotes Billing for Success
-------------------------------------------

Before you can take full advantage of TherapyNotes billing, you need to configure your practice and patient-level billing settings carefully. Skipping these steps is one of the most common reasons practices struggle with denials or incorrect balances later.

### 1\. Practice-Level Billing Settings

Make sure the following items are set up in your account:

*   Practice legal name, tax ID, and billing address
*   NPIs (organizational and individual providers)
*   Accepted insurance payers and payer IDs
*   Default service codes (CPT/HCPCS) and standard rates
*   Electronic claim and ERA enrollment with payers
*   Integrated payment processing, if you plan to store cards on file

  
Accurate global settings ensure that every claim generated through TherapyNotes includes correct organizational and provider identifiers and pricing.

### 2\. Provider and User Roles

TherapyNotes allows you to assign roles such as clinician, supervisor, or biller. For RCM, it is useful to:

*   Assign a dedicated biller role with access to billing reports, claims, and payments
*   Ensure each clinician’s NPI, license info, and taxonomy (if needed) are correct
*   Define who can create or edit charges and who can submit claims

  
Clear roles reduce errors and maintain compliance around who can change financial data.

### 3\. Patient-Level Billing Settings

For each patient, configure:

*   Insurance plans (primary, secondary, etc.) and member IDs
*   Copay, coinsurance, or self-pay arrangements
*   In-network vs. out-of-network status
*   Custom rates or sliding-scale agreements
*   Authorization numbers and visit limits when required

  
Because TherapyNotes billing logic often pulls rates and claim details from patient billing settings, accurate patient profiles are essential for correct charges and fewer denials.

Using TherapyNotes Billing Efficiently
--------------------------------------

Once your configuration is complete, your daily workflow can become much more streamlined. An efficient TherapyNotes billing and RCM cycle usually looks like this:

### 1\. Schedule and Verify

*   Schedule the appointment in TherapyNotes
*   For insured patients, verify eligibility and benefits before the visit when possible
*   Confirm authorizations and visit counts for services that require pre-approval

### 2\. Document and Generate Charges

*   Complete the progress note or session note promptly after the visit
*   Ensure diagnosis codes (ICD-10-CM) and service codes (CPT/HCPCS) are accurate and consistent with documentation
*   Allow the system to generate charges from the appointment and note, adjusting modifiers or units if needed

  
Because documentation, scheduling, and TherapyNotes billing are integrated, finished notes can be directly tied to charges, which reduces manual data entry.

### 3\. Submit Claims and Post Payments

*   Review claim queues for completeness and errors before submission
*   Submit electronic claims in batches for efficiency
*   When ERAs are received, apply payments and adjustments, then move any patient responsibility to statements or card-on-file

  
A disciplined daily or twice-weekly routine keeps A/R moving and reduces the risk of forgotten claims or unposted payments.

Best Practices for Revenue Cycle Management with TherapyNotes
-------------------------------------------------------------

To get the most out of TherapyNotes billing, think in terms of a complete RCM cycle rather than isolated tasks.

Key best practices include:

*   Standardize coding and documentation:

*   *   Use consistent CPT and ICD-10-CM codes for common services
    *   Maintain templates aligned with payer rules to support medical necessity

*   Monitor denials and rejections:

*   *   Track why claims are denied (eligibility, coding, authorization, etc.)
    *   Update workflows or templates to address recurring patterns

*   Leverage reports:

*   *   Monitor days in A/R, collections by payer, write-offs, and no-show impact
    *   Compare clinician productivity and reimbursement to identify bottlenecks

*   Use card-on-file and automated reminders:
    *   Reduce patient bad debt by securely storing cards for copays and balances
    *   Use appointment and balance reminders to improve collections and reduce no-shows

  
When combined, these practices help you turn TherapyNotes billing into a reliable, predictable revenue engine instead of a reactive process.

AI and Automation in TherapyNotes Billing and RCM
-------------------------------------------------

AI around TherapyNotes is evolving, and while specific tools and pricing can change, a few consistent trends are worth integrating into your billing and RCM strategy:

### AI-assisted documentation:

Tools that can generate draft progress notes or summaries from clinician input or session data help clinicians close notes faster. The faster notes are completed, the faster charges can flow into TherapyNotes billing, which shortens the revenue cycle.

### Template-driven, structured notes:

Structured documentation templates make it easier for any AI assistant or rule-based engine to validate codes, capture required elements, and reduce the risk of missing information that might trigger denials.

### Smart data extraction:

Some AI-enabled workflows can help extract plan details or demographic information from insurance cards or intake forms, reducing manual entry errors at the front end of the revenue cycle.

### Claim and denial analytics:

Even if not branded as “AI,” analytics and rules engines can flag unusual patterns, such as high denial rates for a specific payer or code so your billing team can intervene early.

For a small or mid-size behavioral health practice, pairing TherapyNotes billing with these AI-supported workflows can significantly reduce administrative workload and improve collection rates.

When to Consider Outsourcing RCM While Still Using TherapyNotes
---------------------------------------------------------------

Not every practice wants to manage billing internally, even with a solid system. If your clinicians are overwhelmed or your denial rates are high, you can still use TherapyNotes billing while partnering with an external RCM company.

Outsourcing can make sense when:

*   Claims and A/R are falling behind regularly
*   Denial rates stay high despite internal efforts
*   You are scaling quickly and need expert billing capacity
*   You want deeper payer contract analysis and underpayment detection

  
In this model, your practice continues to document and schedule in TherapyNotes, while your billing partner handles claim submission, follow-up, and reporting using the platform’s billing features.

Used strategically, TherapyNotes billing and RCM tools can transform your back office from a source of stress into a powerful support for sustainable growth.
