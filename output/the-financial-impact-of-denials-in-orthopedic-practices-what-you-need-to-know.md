Financial Impact of Denials on Orthopedic Practices:
----------------------------------------------------

Claim denials pose a considerable threat to the financial stability of orthopedic practices. When a claim is denied, it not only delays reimbursement but also increases administrative costs due to the need for reworking and resubmitting claims.

According to the Healthcare Financial Management Association (HFMA), a denial rate exceeding 5% can severely restrict cash flow, impacting the ability to cover day-to-day expenses, employee salaries, and patient care.

4 Most Common Causes of Denials in Orthopedic Practices:
--------------------------------------------------------

1.  **Coding and Modifier Errors**: Incorrect coding or mismatched diagnosis and procedure codes are frequent reasons for denials. Ensuring that coding accurately reflects the services provided is crucial.
2.  **Uncovered Services**: Payers may deny claims for services deemed not medically necessary or not covered under the patient’s insurance plan.
3.  **Prior Authorization Issues**: Failure to obtain necessary prior authorizations can result in claim denials. It’s essential to follow payer guidelines closely to avoid this issue.
4.  **Timely Filing**: Submitting claims outside the payer’s specified timeframe can lead to automatic denials. Ensuring timely submission and resubmission is key.

Top 5 Strategies for Effective Denial Management
------------------------------------------------

### 1\. Accurate Documentation:

Ensuring complete and precise documentation is essential to prevent claim denials. Detailed records provide the necessary support to justify the services rendered and ensure that coding accurately reflects the treatments provided. #### Common CPT Codes:

*   **99213**: Office or other outpatient visit for the evaluation and management of an established patient, typically 15 minutes.
*   **20610**: Arthrocentesis, aspiration, and/or injection; major joint or bursa (e.g., shoulder, hip, knee joint).
*   **29881**: Arthroscopy, knee, surgical; with meniscectomy (medial OR lateral, including any meniscal shaving).
*   **23412**: Repair of ruptured musculotendinous cuff (e.g., rotator cuff) open; chronic.
*   **27236**: Open treatment of femoral fracture, proximal end, neck, internal fixation or prosthetic replacement.
