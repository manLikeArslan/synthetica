Understanding Government Regulations in OBGYN Credentialing
-----------------------------------------------------------

Credentialing timelines and payer policies often align with federal regulations set by the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS). CMS ensures that all healthcare providers meet required standards before participating in government-funded insurance programs.

For OBGYN practices, this includes:

**Medicare Credentialing** — Essential for serving the growing population of women in Medicare age ranges or those with Medicare due to disability

**Medicaid Credentialing** — Critical for obstetrical care, as Medicaid covers approximately 42% of all births in the United States

**CLIA Certification** — Required if your practice performs any laboratory testing, including common OBGYN tests

**CMS Price Transparency Requirements** — Ensuring your practice meets disclosure requirements for procedures and services

**Federal Reference:** For detailed CMS credentialing requirements, visit the official [CMS Provider Enrollment](https://www.cms.gov/medicare/enrollment-renewal/providers-suppliers/enrollment-applications-and-forms) page.

You’ll need medical school transcripts, residency completion certificates, state medical licenses, DEA certificates, malpractice insurance documentation, hospital privilege letters, and notarized evidence of performing a minimum of 50 major obstetrical and gynecologic operations. Our team guides you through exactly what’s needed and helps obtain any missing documentation.
