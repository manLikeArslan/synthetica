Understanding Adjudication in Medical Billing
---------------------------------------------

The evaluation of the claims to check their effectiveness and accuracy is called adjudication in medical billing. The payer assesses the claim for the patient’s information to ensure accuracy. Likewise, it reviews the medical codes using the Current Procedural Terminology (CPT codes) and the Healthcare Common Procedure Coding System (HCPCS).  
Moving forward, the payer reviews the legality of the services rendered. For example, if the rendered service is for podiatry, then the payer will evaluate the medical codes of **[podiatry medical billing](https://docsmedicalbilling.com/common-mistakes-in-podiatry-medical-billing-and-how-to-avoid-it/)** to verify the claim. After reviewing all the requirements, the payer either accepts the claims or denies them due to inaccuracy.

Adjudication Procedure
----------------------

### Evaluation

The first step in the adjudication is the evaluation of the claims submitted by the healthcare provider. The payer checks the patient’s/provider’s information, status, and services. This step is crucial for verifying the claims and avoiding any discrepancies.

### Validation

The second step of adjudication in medical billing is to identify the patient’s coverage and insurance validity. It ensures that the claims and services align with the patient’s insurance health plan. The validation is automated, and then the expert medical examiner cross-checks the information.

### Decision

After the complete evaluation, the payor makes the decision regarding claims. It either rejects the claim due to missing or inaccurate information or reimburses it as per the insurance policy. The payor provides a valid reason for denying the claim, usually through a Remittance Advice (RA) or Explanation of Benefits (EOB). After correcting the claim, the provider appeals for an **[AR recovery process](https://docsmedicalbilling.com/what-is-the-ar-recovery-process-in-healthcare-billing/)**.

### Payment

After accepting the claim, the insurance company pays the healthcare provider for the services rendered. In addition, there’s a specific amount that the patient has to pay out-of-pocket for the treatment. The payer will not pay this patient’s responsibility.

Optimization of Claim Adjudication in Medical Billing
-----------------------------------------------------

It is advisable for providers to submit clean claims. So, let’s see how optimization of claims adjudication in medical billing is done:

### Enhance Automation

Payers can invest in automation to reduce manual adjudication work. By using modern technology, the system helps in reducing billing errors. Furthermore, it prevents duplication, evaluates patient and service information, and monitors the patient’s health plan.

### Coding Evaluation

It is necessary for payers to evaluate medical codes using the Current Procedural Terminology (CPT codes) and the Healthcare Common Procedure Coding System (HCPCS). Medical codes are crucial because they help identify the correct medical entity. Therefore, stay updated with codes to prevent prior authorization issues and missing information.

### Improve Data Management

One of the most common reasons behind claim denial is inaccurate and missing data. For example, if the code mentioned in the claim doesn’t match the service rendered, then the payer rejects the claim. It happens due to poor data management. Therefore, implement automation to enhance data management.

### Efficient Patient Communication

The next strategy for optimizing claim adjudication in medical billing is patient-provider collaboration. Connect with patients to collect their information. In addition, they can use automation to enter the information so that the system automatically corrects it on every visit.

### Standardize Claims Adjudication

To achieve **[revenue optimization in healthcare](https://docsmedicalbilling.com/revenue-optimization-in-healthcare/)**, it is crucial to submit error-free claims. A clean claim is the foundation of efficient financial health. It can happen by following the adjudication standardization set by the payer.
