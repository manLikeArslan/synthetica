### **1\. Misclassification of Procedures as Cosmetic vs. Reconstructive**

#### **The Issue:**

Insurance carriers may deny coverage if a procedure appears cosmetic, even when it’s medically necessary. Misclassification is one of the leading reasons for denied claims in plastic surgery.

#### **Common Confusions:**

*   Breast reduction vs. augmentation
*   Functional rhinoplasty vs. cosmetic reshaping
*   Scar revision following injury vs. elective enhancement

#### **MBC’s Solution:**

We ensure procedures are properly categorized, with supporting documentation such as operative notes, diagnosis codes, and payer-specific pre-approvals that meet medical necessity criteria.

### **2\. Preauthorization Errors or Omissions**

#### **The Issue:**

Many medically necessary plastic surgery procedures require prior authorization. Failing to secure or document this can result in a complete denial, even if the service was covered.

#### **High-Risk Procedures:**

*   Septoplasty
*   Reconstructive breast surgery
*   Skin grafting or tissue expansion

#### **MBC’s Solution:**

Our team tracks authorization requirements by payer and ensures every necessary step is completed before the claim is submitted, minimizing delays and denials.

### **3\. Improper Coding and Modifier Use**

#### **The Issue:**

Plastic surgery often involves multiple services performed in one session. Incorrect use of CPT codes or missing modifiers (like 59, 62, or 25) can lead to underpayment or denials.

#### **Common Coding Challenges:**

*   Bundled services
*   Bilateral procedures
*   Add-on codes and co-surgeon modifiers

#### **MBC’s Solution:**

We employ certified coders who specialize in **Plastic Surgery Billing**. Claims are pre-audited to ensure modifier accuracy, compliant code sequencing, and maximum allowable reimbursement.

### **4\. Missed Charges and Incomplete Billing**

#### **The Issue:**

Busy plastic surgery teams often miss opportunities to bill separately for certain services, especially those performed outside of surgery or post-operatively.

#### **Examples of Missed Revenue:**

*   Wound care
*   Suture removal (outside the global period)
*   Supplies or injections are billed separately

#### **MBC’s Solution:**

We perform routine charge capture audits and educate your staff on identifying every billable component of care. Nothing slips through the cracks.

### **5\. Poor Handling of Self-Pay and Cosmetic Billing**

#### **The Issue:**

Many elective procedures are paid for directly by patients. When costs aren’t clearly communicated—or collection procedures are inconsistent—this can lead to disputes or bad debt.

#### **Common Risks:**

*   Inaccurate or missing estimates
*   No deposit or pre-payment policies
*   Surprise billing after the service

#### **MBC’s Solution:**

We help practices implement structured self-pay workflows, from cost estimates to installment plans. Our team also trains your staff to communicate clearly with patients and set proper financial expectations.
