What is The Credentialing Process in Healthcare?
------------------------------------------------

Credentialing, also known as provider enrollment, guarantees that a medical professional is qualified to practice and provide patient care. A provider can quickly get enrolled and approved through this procedure through a credentialing committee. It gives surety that the education, medical training, certifications, and licensing are authentic. Thus, a complete history check of a healthcare professional is done to practice medical services in the USA, called credentialing.

Types of Medical Credentialing Process
--------------------------------------

Credentialing in medical billing is of three types. Let’s discuss them one by one and decide which type you need.

### Initial Credentialing

Also known as enrollment in healthcare, initial credentialing is for new healthcare providers. In this procedure, we’ll help you verify your qualifications and enroll you so that you can begin your practice within a healthcare organization.

### Re-Credentialing

If the provider has already undergone the verification and enrollment process, they might need to re-verify all the documentation. Re-credentialing is like updating your existing provider’s profile.

### Provisional Credentialing

Also called temporary credentialing, the providers need to undergo temporary credentialing, especially those new to the medical organization. Through provisional credentialing, they can start their practice while permanent credentialing is underway.

Essential Steps For Credentialing in Medical Billing
----------------------------------------------------

A physician must follow the following steps to complete the credentialing process.

### Identification of Credentials

The first and crucial step is to identify and verify all the documents. It includes verification of educational documents, work history, license, board certification, hospital affiliations or privileges, and references from supervisors or colleagues.

### Verify Primary sources

Verifying all the documentation from primary sources is crucial, as is calling institutions and organizations where the provider claims to obtain credentials, certifications, and licensing. All the claims need verification to prove they meet the set standards of the provider’s qualifications. These documents include:

*   Medical school certificates
*   Medical licenses
*   Board approvals
*   Residency and fellowship training
*   Previous working details (for experienced providers)

### Submission of Credentialing Application

When the information is gathered successfully, verifying all the information and documents is time. Afterward, the documents are submitted to an insurance company. A provider can submit it or consult a third-party agency to perform this task. DocsMed saves healthcare providers from the headache of applying and helps simplify the credentialing process. Likewise, it helps them prevent reimbursement delays.

### Credentialing Committee Review

A medical credentialing committee is set to review all the provider’s credentials. A comprehensive check is done to verify that the provider is capable of providing patient care. Additionally, a panel of experienced professionals checks for malpractice history and all the relevant information. This panel holds the authority to stop provider credentialing if any information is missing or incorrect. Once your information is complete and correct, the committee will approve your eligibility and pass your credentialing.
