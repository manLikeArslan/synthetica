**What are ASC Claim Rejections?**
----------------------------------

When your insurance company refuses to pay your medical claim, it is known as an **ASC claim rejection**.

If there is a mistake in an ASC claim submission, your insurance provider will reject it. After that, you receive a copy of the claim back along with information about the errors so you may fix it and submit it again.

**What Are the Most Common ASC Claims Submission Errors?**
----------------------------------------------------------

 Healthcare providers like yours must understand the causes for rejected claims and rejections and endeavor to reduce them, as they can result in:

*   The payments do not arrive on schedule.
*   Administrative costs have increased.
*   Reduced revenue.

When submitting ASC claims, a variety of problems may arise. Reduce claims filing mistakes by paying close attention to detail and carefully evaluating all information supplied.

**Some common errors:**
-----------------------

**Common Errors**

**Description**

Incorrect patient information

Errors in patient information such as name, birth date, or insurance policy number might result in claim rejections or denials.

Incomplete or missing information

Denials can occur due to missing or insufficient information, such as diagnostic or procedure codes, resulting in coding mistakes.

Incorrect coding

Using erroneous procedures or diagnostic codes may result in claim denials or inaccurate payments from insurance companies.

Non-covered services

Non-covered services under the patient’s insurance policy can lead to claim denials in ASC billing. Practices should verify patient coverage beforehand.

Duplicate ASC billing

Submitting several bills for the same service might result in denials and administrative complications for both the provider and the insurer.

Untimely filing

Claims submission must be within a deadline to prevent refusal. The timing varies according to the patient’s insurance plan.

Coordination of benefits (COB) errors

COB errors occur when multiple insurance plans cover a patient, and providers fail to bill correctly.
