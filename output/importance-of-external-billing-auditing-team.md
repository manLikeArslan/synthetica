# Importance of External Billing Auditing Team

*Original URL: https://docsmedicalbilling.com/importance-of-external-billing-auditing-team/*

---

The finance team conducts internal audits to analyze a medical practice’s financial performance. Likewise, audits scrutinize financial performance to prevent billing errors and optimize revenue cycles. Although internal audits are essential for the revenue cycle, consulting an external billing auditing team is still helpful. It provides more objective results by identifying mistakes that may be overlooked during internal audits. Furthermore, it ensures regulatory compliance and adherence to industry regulations. Thus, a provider can outsource **[medical billing audit services](https://docsmedicalbilling.com/medical-billing-audit-services-in-usa/)** for a comprehensive financial analysis.

Understanding External Billing Audits
-------------------------------------

An external auditor helps healthcare providers understand the billing and coding errors by reviewing them independently. The external billing auditing team consists of medical billing, coding, and compliance experts. Likewise, the professional team reviews the practice billing, including documentation, the claim process, **[payment posting](https://docsmedicalbilling.com/understanding-payment-posting-in-medical-billing/)**, and all the relevant processes. Their objective approach helps to identify all the billing mistakes that impact the overall revenue cycle. The following are the different types of audits that a provider can outsource externally:

Focused Audit: The auditors focus on a specific subset of the organization and analyze its performance.

**Compliance Audits:** The team analyzes the practice’s compliance with Medicare, Medicaid, HIPAA, and other private payers through compliance audits.

**Pre-Payment Audits:** The team audits claims before submitting them to payers to ensure they are accurate with proper CPT, HCPCS, or ICD-10 codes.

**Post Payment Audits:** The experts identify the potential reimbursement issues after the claim is submitted and reimbursed.  
**Billing and Coding Audits:** The audit team reviews every step of the process, from patient registration to reimbursement, to detect possible billing and coding mistakes.

Importance of External Billing Auditing Team
--------------------------------------------

An external billing auditing team enables healthcare providers to streamline the billing process by identifying potential mistakes causing revenue leakages. The experts help maximize cash flow and reimbursement by optimizing financial performance. Furthermore, they review the **[AR recovery process](https://docsmedicalbilling.com/what-is-the-ar-recovery-process-in-healthcare-billing/)** to identify whether claims are being processed timely to the payer. The most important thing about external auditors is that they give suggestions based on unbiased evaluation.

How Do Experts Conduct Audits in Medical Billing?
-------------------------------------------------

### Audit Planning

The auditors start by checking if it is an investigative, preparatory, or routine audit. Likewise, the team analyzes the specific areas or regions that need auditing. It further checks the necessity of documentation, billing, and coding records, and types of claims.

### Data Collection

Data collection includes patient records, claims data, documentation, and payment processes. The external billing auditing team uses systematic data collection to evaluate everything comprehensively. Likewise, coding sheets are also required for analysis.

### Medical Record Review

This step includes comparing provider documentation and billed services. The team verifies the medical necessity of the service and treatment provided. In addition, it enables providers to learn about the coding accuracy in the claims.

### Compliance Review

Compliance reviews include following CMS, HIPAA, and private payer guidelines when filing a claim. Auditors review compliance regulations and policy adherence and analyze local and national coding standards.

### Possible Errors

The main goal of the external billing auditing team is to identify possible billing and coding errors. It determines the issues with upcoding, undercoding, unbundling, missing documentation, claim duplication, and non-covered services labeled as covered.

### Reporting and Analytics

After carefully analyzing the pain points during audits, the experts identify the error rates, discrepancies, financial impacts, and suggestions. The real-time reporting and analytics help providers enhance **[revenue optimization in healthcare](https://docsmedicalbilling.com/revenue-optimization-in-healthcare/)**.

### Follow-Up Audits

Auditing doesn’t mean a one-time analysis of healthcare organizations. A provider must hire experts for continuous follow-up audits. Regular auditing helps providers continuously monitor the organization’s financial health and reduces the risk of audits and penalties.

How to Choose the Right External Billing Auditing Team?
-------------------------------------------------------

### Experience

It is crucial to learn about the expertise and experience of external auditors in medical billing and coding. The auditors must have a proven record of auditing medical billing.

### Healthcare Certifications

Healthcare certifications include HIPAA compliance, CPC Coder, CMRS, and CPMA. These certifications show that the auditors are experts and have the skills for thorough auditing.

### Customized Auditing

The external billing auditing team must ensure that it provides customized auditing. For example, if a provider wants auditing of a specific area, they should customize services.

### Use of Advanced Technology

Using advanced technology is a requirement for coding and auditing efficiency. These software programs help auditors update themselves with the latest industry trends.

### Real-Time Reporting

Ask if they provide real-time reporting and analytics after completing audits. Likewise, ask for feedback and suggestions to protect the bottom line.
