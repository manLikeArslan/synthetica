**What’s New in CMS Telehealth Expansion for 2025?**
----------------------------------------------------

The 2025 CMS updates bring several key changes that neurology practices need to know:

1.  **Expanded List of Covered Services**: Telehealth now permanently covers neurology services like cognitive assessments, follow-up evaluations, and specific diagnostic procedures.
2.  **Increased Reimbursement Rates**: Telehealth visits will be reimbursed at rates closer to in-person visits, offering a **15-20% boost** in revenue opportunities for neurology practices.
3.  **Permanent Coverage for Rural and Urban Areas**: Telehealth coverage, once limited to rural settings, now extends to urban locations, broadening access for millions of patients.
4.  **Audio-Only Services**: CMS now recognizes the value of audio-only consultations, which are especially helpful for neurology patients with limited access to video technology.

These changes highlight the importance of optimizing telehealth billing processes to maximize revenue and ensure compliance.

**Billing Best Practices for Telehealth in Neurology**
------------------------------------------------------

To stay ahead in 2025, follow these best practices for telehealth billing:

1.  **Verify Patient Eligibility:** Before scheduling a telehealth appointment, confirm that the patient’s insurance covers the service. Check for specifics like copayments and deductibles to avoid surprises.
2.  **Use Accurate Telehealth CPT Codes:** Selecting the correct Current Procedural Terminology (CPT) codes is critical. Standard telehealth codes for neurology include:
    *   **99201-99215**: Office or outpatient visits
    *   **99453-99454**: Remote monitoring of physiologic data
    *   **G0459**: Telehealth consultations for emergency department visits
    *   Don’t forget to append the appropriate modifiers, such as **95**, for telehealth services.
3.  **Maintain Comprehensive Documentation:** Proper documentation is essential to support accurate billing and reduce audit risks. Be sure to include:
    *   Date and time of the encounter
    *   Patient consent for telehealth
    *   Assessment and treatment plan
    *   Duration of the consultation
4.  **Monitor State-Specific Regulations:** Telehealth regulations vary by state, so stay informed about licensure requirements and billing guidelines specific to your location.

### **Addressing Common Billing Challenges**

1.  **Telehealth Claim Denials:** Denials often stem from incorrect codes, missing modifiers, or documentation errors. Regular audits can help identify and resolve these issues.
2.  **Patient Cost-Sharing Confusion:** Many patients are unclear about their financial responsibilities for telehealth services. Transparent communication about copayments and deductibles upfront can prevent confusion.
3.  **Technological Barriers:** Ensure both providers and patients are comfortable with the telehealth platform. Offering training sessions can minimize technical issues and improve the patient experience.

3.  **Enhanced Patient Engagement:** Telehealth platforms will increasingly incorporate tools for remote patient monitoring and data sharing, improving care coordination and patient satisfaction.

FAQs
----

**1\. What telehealth services in neurology are now covered permanently?**

CMS now permanently covers tele‑neuro services such as cognitive assessments, follow‑ups, and certain diagnostics via telehealth.

**2\. Will telehealth visits pay as well as in‑person neurology visits?**

Yes — reimbursement rates for telehealth visits are increased by about 15‑20%, much closer to in‑office levels.

**3\. Can I bill telehealth from urban areas too?**

Absolutely — CMS has removed rural-only restrictions, making telehealth available and billable in urban settings too.

**4\. Are audio‑only neurology consultations billable?**

Yes — CMS now supports billing for audio‑only consults, especially helpful for patients without video‑capable devices.

**5\. What are key tips for successful tele‑neuro billing?**

Use correct CPT codes (e.g. 99201–99215, 99453–99454), include telehealth modifier 95, and document consent, time, and treatment details.
