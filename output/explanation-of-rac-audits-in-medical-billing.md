# Explanation of RAC Audits in Medical Billing

*Original URL: https://docsmedicalbilling.com/explanation-of-rac-audits-in-medical-billing/*

---

Recovering already spent revenue due to improper payments is a challenging task in medical billing. The reimbursed claims may have billing discrepancies, which will be identified in Recovery Audit Contractor audits, also known as RAC audits. Likewise, CMS puts effort into streamlining the billing and revenue process by hiring auditing experts. These experts analyze the medical data of the last three (03) years. It will help detect the underpayments and overpayments billed to providers and healthcare organizations. Additionally, providers can outsource **[medical billing audit services](https://docsmedicalbilling.com/medical-billing-audit-services-in-usa/)** to identify potential billing errors. An outsourced audit team enables providers to protect themselves from penalties and recoupments.

What is RAC Audit?
------------------

It is the audit conducted by the Recovery Audit Contractor that performs an in-depth financial analysis of the practice to identify past payment errors. It was first introduced in 2005 by the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid to improve the overall payment collections. Similarly, CMS hires RACs on a contingency fee basis to resolve payment issues, such as underpayments or overpayments. The contingency fee basis refers to the payment Medicare reimburses to auditors based on the percentage of payment issues they collect. In addition, they are allowed to conduct a review of the last 3 years of medical data.

Purpose of RAC Audits
---------------------

The purpose of RAC audits is to identify revenue ambiguities from the past three years. It examines the past reimbursed payments, which include providers’ fees and administrative costs. Likewise, any disorientation in payments is resolved by applying recoupment, in which the payer will deduct an amount from future reimbursements. Regular audits streamline the billing workflow and optimize cash flow.

Types of RAC Audits
-------------------

### Automated

It is the type of audit that doesn’t need additional documents to perform financial analysis. Typically, it detects the claims that are not under the policies. For example, a provider bills the services under an incorrect code. Automated audits will detect the error and recommend improvements.

### Complex

It requires a more detailed review of the overall collections, which necessitates extensive supporting documentation. The documents must verify the medical necessity of the treatment in the claim. Similarly, it includes date/place of service, treatment type, procedure complexity, and other essential information.

Impact of Improper Payments on Healthcare Providers
---------------------------------------------------

### Recoupment Implementation

Recoupment is a process in which the payer demands the return of an already reimbursed excess amount to balance the financial gap. After RAC audits, CMS may implement recoupment to require providers to return excessive funds.

### Increased Administrative Burden

Auditing increases the administrative burden on providers because they need to submit detailed documentation. The medical services rendered should be comprehensively explained to prove the medical necessity of the treatment, resulting in an increased administrative burden.

### Financial Complexity

Frequent underpayments and overpayments disturb the practice’s revenue cycle. It creates billing discrepancies, which further impact cash flow. Furthermore, other reasons include incorrect coding and errors in claims, which can lead to financial complexity.

Conclusion
----------

To summarize, RAC audits in medical billing help reduce revenue discrepancies and streamline the billing workflow. The audits identify revenue complexities by identifying underpayments and overpayments of the past 3 years. CMS can use the recoupment method to balance the improper payments or medical funds. Thus, providing extensive details of medical necessity through accurate documentation can help providers avoid audit penalties.
