What You Need to Know About AR Follow-Up in Medical Billing?
------------------------------------------------------------

The money a patient and payer owe to a healthcare provider for services rendered is called accounts receivable. Similarly, AR management is a complex process. The AR follow-up in medical billing manages the pending payments and denied claims to ensure easy payment flow. AR experts ensure that the revenue cycle management remains intact and balances are seamlessly transferred to the providers without any discrepancies.

Why is AR Follow-Up in Medical Billing Important?
-------------------------------------------------

Behind a successful medical practice, the maintenance of positive cash flow is essential. Likewise, the AR follow-up team ensures the provider receives timely payment for patient care and treatment services. Managing accounts receivable is following up with the payer and patient to reopen the denied claims and recover overdue payment. As a result, it leads to successful claims submission and rightful reimbursements.

### Evaluation

The first phase of the AR follow-up in medical billing is the evaluation of the accounts receivable reports. The AR team reviews all the pending payments and searches for the reasons behind denials. Furthermore, the team reviews the insurance policies to identify the reasons for every failed payment.

### Analysis and Prioritization of Denied Claims

The second step is to analyze the denied claims and prioritize them for payment at the earliest. This step further lists the payments that have not yet been made per the contract. Moreover, a healthcare organization can contact experts by outsourcing medical billing services.

### Collection

After thoroughly evaluating, analyzing, and prioritizing, the collection phase begins. The billing team creates patient bills and submits them to the payer with all details. Once the payment posting process is complete, the team reaches out to patients and payers to ensure timely payments. As a result, healthcare providers receive complete and on-time reimbursements.
